## TL;DR

**HIGH-PROBABILITY MATCH for the Rapaport family's Brussels → Israel transport.** The ship Theodor Herzl (תיאודור הרצל) explicitly collected passengers by train from Belgium, sailed from Sète (southern France) on 2 April 1947, was intercepted by British destroyers off Tel Aviv on 13 April 1947, and its passengers were deported to Cyprus detention camps. They were released to Israel after the state was founded in May 1948.

Every known data point about our family aligns:

| Family fact | Theodor Herzl fact |
|---|---|
| Brussels DP registration April 1946 (DP card) | Some passengers boarded by train **from Belgium** (Hebrew Wikipedia, quoted) |
| Dov Bernard born Brussels 1946 (family oral) | 500 children + youth among 2,620 total passengers |
| Family memory: "Cyprus internment camps" | Survivors deported to Cyprus detention camps after April 1947 interception |
| Family memory: arrived Israel post-1948 | Cyprus detainees released after Israeli statehood (May 1948) |

This is the **strongest single-ship fit** for our family's known transport pattern.

## What is CONFIRMED about Theodor Herzl

Sources: [Hebrew Wikipedia — תיאודור הרצל (אוניית מעפילים)](https://he.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%90%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%A8_%D7%94%D7%A8%D7%A6%D7%9C_(%D7%90%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99%D7%99%D7%AA_%D7%9E%D7%A2%D7%A4%D7%99%D7%9C%D7%99%D7%9D)), [Palyam — Theodor Herzl voyage PDF](https://www.palyam.org/English/Hahapala/hf/hf_Theodor_Herzl.pdf), [USHMM Holocaust Encyclopedia](https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/photo/jewish-refugees-from-the-aliyah-bet-ship-theodor-herzl)

- **Embarkation**: Port of Sète, France, 2 April 1947 at 06:45
- **Passengers**: 2,620 (some sources say 2,641) Holocaust survivors and Zionist youth movement members
- **Verbatim from Wikipedia**: *"חלקם הובאו ברכבת מבלגיה"* — *"some were brought by train from Belgium"*
- Additional passengers came from France and other European locations, concentrated in Provence DP camps
- Demographic: roughly one-third young pregnant women; 500 children/teens under the supervision of Chasia Biletzky; predominant age range 20-30
- **Interception**: 13 April 1947, by HMS Haydon and HMS St Brides Bay near Tel Aviv coast
- **Casualties**: 3 passengers killed, 27+ wounded in violent resistance
- **Deportation**: ~2,600 sent to **Cyprus** detention camps; sick/wounded to Atlit camp on the mainland
- **Release**: Most reached Israel after state declared 14 May 1948

## How well does the family pattern fit?

### What aligns strongly
- **Brussels → train → Sète boarding**: The Wikipedia explicit Belgian train route is the smoking-gun match. There were many Aliyah Bet ships but few that explicitly collected Belgian passengers.
- **Timing**: April 1946 Brussels DP → ~12 months in Brussels (Dov born) → April 1947 train south → boarding → Cyprus → state-founding May 1948 → release. Each leg fits cleanly.
- **Cyprus internment**: Family memory is specific about Cyprus, not Atlit. Theodor Herzl's main passenger destination was Cyprus.

### Caveats / unknowns
- David's age 35 at boarding (b. 1911 + ~36 yrs by April 1947) is **older than the stated 20-30 median** — but Wikipedia says "approximately" and notes pregnant women + children, implying full families were on board.
- Baby Dov would have been ~1 year old at boarding. Plausible. The 500-child group fits.
- Leah's age 34 (if 1913 DOB) or 31 (if 1916) also fits family demographic.
- Three passengers were killed in the British boarding — we have no record this happened to our family, but it's worth knowing the violence was real.

### What would falsify this
- A Rapaport family ON a different specific ship (e.g. Exodus 1947, Geulah, etc.) in the same window
- Israel arrival date significantly earlier than mid-1948 (before Cyprus releases began)
