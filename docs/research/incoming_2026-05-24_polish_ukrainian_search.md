But this is speculation; the Yizkor doesn't name his parents.

### Yizkor Book Holocaust sections

Searched the Przemyśl Yizkor's Holocaust pages (prz371, prz394, prz490) for: Rapaport, Schiff, Griffel, Berisz, Bernard, Rebeka, Regina, Lota, railway/kolej, bomb, sabotage.

**No matches.** The destruction narrative covers Soviet occupation 1939-41, German takeover June 1941, Janowska deportations June 1941, property confiscation, ghetto creation July 14 1942, and the August/September 1942 deportations to Bełżec — but does NOT name individual victims of early Gestapo executions or railway-sabotage accusations.

### German Wikipedia [Judenverfolgung in Przemyśl](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judenverfolgung_in_Przemy%C5%9Bl)

Timeline of German occupation after 28 June 1941 — registration, Judenrat (Ignaz Duldig), 1000-Jew Janowska deportation on day one, forced labor, property seizure, ghetto in Garbarze district July 14 1942. **No mention of Rapaport family or railway-bomb incident.**

### Polish history article: "[Afisze śmierci i pierwsza egzekucja w Przemyślu — listopad 1943](https://www.przemyskiehistorie.pl/afisze-smierci-egzekucja-przemysl-listopad-1943-rok/)"

**Critical finding**: The first **publicly-posted** German execution in Przemyśl took place 29 November 1943 — eighteen months after Lusia's memoir places Berisz's death. The article documents five hostage lists posted 1943-1944, with ~120 names total. **No Rapaport in those lists.**

→ **Interpretation**: Berisz's late-1941 / early-1942 execution would have been an INDIVIDUAL action, before the systematic public-hostage poster campaign began. This is consistent with Lusia's account ("the Germans killed her father-in-law, accusing him of allegedly placing a bomb on the railway near which they lived") — likely an arrest by Gestapo or SD, summary execution, no public poster. Such individual deaths typically don't survive in archive aggregates; they'd only be found in:
- Individual Gestapo death-card registers (Sicherheitspolizei records — most destroyed)
- Polish IPN dossiers if a survivor filed retroactively
- Yad Vashem Pages of Testimony filed by family

## Two verified Przemyśl Rapaports the family didn't know about

These are not Berisz, but they ARE documented Rapaport residents of Przemyśl:

1. **Rabbi Shimon Rappoport** — a HaMizrachi (Religious Zionist) leader. Worth investigating if he could be a kin connection to our family — same surname, same city.
2. **Szabse / Shabtai Rappaport** — b.~1908, d.~1942 age 33, Hebrew Gymnasium teacher in Przemyśl, published Hebrew-Hasidic-psychology scholar. If he was a young nephew or first cousin of Berisz on the paternal side, this would be a meaningful family lead. He published in Baderech, Gilionot, and the 1930 Warsaw Yearbook of Jewish Writers — bibliographic searches could surface his bio + parentage.
