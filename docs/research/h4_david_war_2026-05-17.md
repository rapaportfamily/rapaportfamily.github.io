3. **Germans took over from Soviets June 1941**; mass aktion at Bukowinka Forest 6 Oct 1941 murdered 2,000+ Jews ([Yad Vashem Nadwórna](https://www.yadvashem.org/communities/nadworna.html))
4. **Nadwórna ghetto formed April 1942** with ~3,600 Jews ([Yad Vashem Nadwórna ghetto](https://www.yadvashem.org/communities/nadworna/ghetto-liquidation.html))
5. **Ghetto liquidated 24 Oct 1942** — Jews forced into synagogue, plundered, murdered. Few craftsmen survived in Ghetto A until Nov 1942, then executed
6. **Anders Army formed in USSR Aug 1941** (Sikorski-Mayski agreement) after Soviet release of Polish deportees ([Anders' Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anders%27_Army))
7. **Anders Army evacuated to Iran Aug 1942** (~110,000 Poles + ~5,000-6,000 Jews); fought via Italy 1943-45
8. **Pre-war Nadwórna had a Jewish wood treatment factory employing Jews including hachshara members** (Yad Vashem) — supports the family-forestry context

## The two plausible paths (NEITHER yet confirmed)

### Path A — Soviet deportation → Anders Army → Italy → West

Evidence FOR:
- David was in Soviet-occupied Lwów with a forestry profession — exact profile Soviets deported in April 1940
- The DP card "1942 = citizenship stripped" matches the year Polish citizens lost Soviet-imposed status when Sikorski-Mayski agreement collapsed
- Anders Army Italian campaign (1944-45) → demobilization in Western Europe → fits Brussels arrival
- **David's Italian fluency is a striking clue** — where would a Galician forester learn fluent Italian? Anders Army Italian campaign is one plausible explanation
- The DP card was filed in Brussels (French-speaking) but it lists Italian as a language he speaks — suggesting prior Italian exposure

Evidence AGAINST:
- Family oral history says he escaped Nadwórna ghetto in 1942 (would mean he was THERE in 1942, not in Iran)
- No Polish military record yet checked — could confirm or refute

### Path B — Stayed in German Galicia → ghetto escape → hiding in Lwów → liberation → Brussels

Evidence FOR:
- Family oral history is consistent and specific (railway wagon, logs, Lwów, false papers)
- Ghetto liquidation date (24 Oct 1942) aligns with the DP card "1942 = stripped of citizenship" — Nazi-era deprivation
- His specific forestry knowledge would have given access to timber transport (logical fit for the escape route)
- Some Jews DID escape Nadwórna ghetto to forests / Hungary (Yad Vashem confirms)

Evidence AGAINST:
- Italian fluency is much harder to explain on this path
- Brussels arrival route from Lwów in 1944-46 less straightforward than Italy → West
- "Railway/road construction in Lwów" — if he was caught working, he'd be in a labor camp, not free in Brussels by April 1946

### Path C — A combination

Some Jewish deportees were released by Soviets in 1941, made their way back to family in German-occupied Galicia, and then survived the ghetto. Or the inverse: ghetto escape → joined Soviet partisans → ended up west.

**No invented narrative here.** This is logically possible but evidence-free.
