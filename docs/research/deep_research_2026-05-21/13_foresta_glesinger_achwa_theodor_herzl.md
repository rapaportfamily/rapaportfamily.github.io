## Foresta company — confirmed in three independent sources

Foresta was the Jewish-owned, Jewish-managed timber-lease operation in Nadwórna that became the wartime sawmill where David worked.

Source 1 — Yad Vashem Nadwórna pre-war page (https://www.yadvashem.org/communities/nadworna/before-holocaust.html):
> "The 'Foresta' company was active in the city, leasing woodlands from the Polish authorities in order to chop timber and prepare it for export. Both the managers of the company in the city and its workers were Jewish."
>
> "In 1937, the government contract with the 'Foresta' company – whose owners and workers in Nadwórna were Jewish – was terminated, due to interference by antisemitic Ukrainians and Poles."

Source 2 — Pinkas Hakehillot Polin Vol. 2 — Nadvirna entry (https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/pinkas_poland/pol2_00328.html):
> Confirms Foresta was nationalized by Soviets after September 1939; Jews retained managerial positions.

Source 3 — Yad Vashem under-occupation page (https://www.yadvashem.org/communities/nadworna/german-occupation.html):
> "approximately 600 Jews worked daily at the sawmill in an attempt to obtain permits classifying them as 'essential workers.'"

⇒ **Foresta = the wartime Nadwórna sawmill where David held his "essential worker" badge.**

⇒ **Foresta owners' names are NOT on the web.** The full Pinkas Hakehillot Polin Hebrew text (longer than the JewishGen English summary) is needed. Search the Hebrew for "בעלי פורסטה" ("owners of Foresta").

## Glesinger timber dynasty — NOT the same as Foresta

The earlier dossier speculation that the Nadwórna sawmill was a Glesinger property turns out to be incorrect.

Source — Rozhnyatów Yizkor Book pp.17-20 (https://www.jewishgen.org/Yizkor/Rozniatow/roz017.html):
> "Egon Glesinger was born into a very rich Jewish family with immense holdings of forests and related wood industries in Teschen, a region on both sides of the boundary between Poland and Czechoslovakia."
>
> "The sawmills of Broszniow-Krechowice of Glesingers and the oil fields at Rypne and Dubno provided much employment for the region, including Jews."
>
> "The entire region enjoyed the free fare on two narrow-gauge railway lines operated by the Glesingers for the transportation of logs from the forests, deep in the Carpathian mountains to the sawmills."

Source — FAO Unasylva In Memoriam Egon Glesinger (https://www.fao.org/4/l5470e/l5470e07.htm):
> "Egon was born in 1905 and was the son of Emil Glesinger and Jolán Heller... In the academic year 1930/31 Egon was completing his doctoral thesis on the European forestry industry, 'Le bois en Europe', at the Institut Universitaire de Hautes études Internationales in Geneva. He succeeded in forming the Comité international du bois in the 1930s... Egon was Director of the Forestry and Forest Products Division [of the FAO] from 1959 to 1963."

⇒ The Glesinger empire was centered in **Rozhnyatów / Broszniów** (Stanyslaviv district, ~70km north-west of Nadwórna), NOT Nadwórna itself. Foresta and Glesinger were **distinct** Jewish timber operations in the same Galician region.

Egon Glesinger's 1942 book "Nazis in the Woodpile" (Bobbs-Merrill, 262 pp.) is still worth reading for general context on Galician timber Aryanisation but doesn't name Nadwórna specifically.

## Theodor Herzl voyage — full crew + 3 killed + 7 named passengers

Source — Palyam ship history (https://www.palyam.org/English/Hahapala/hf/hf_Theodor_Herzl.pdf):
> "The vessel set sail on April 2nd 1947, from the port of Sete, France, carrying 2,641 Ma'apilim."
>
> "Commander: Mecca Limon, accompanied by Yosh Halvei, Betzalel (Tzolo) Feldman and Chaim Wineshelboim. The Gideoni was Nachman (Bob) Burstein."
>
> "Prepared for its voyage by the Marseilles team of Geda Shochat and Nachum Carmeli-Shamir."
>
> "On 13 April 1947, the Theodor Herzl (2,641 passengers) was intercepted by HMS Haydon and HMS St Brides Bay. Passengers resisted heavily; three were killed and 27 were injured."
>
> "three Ma'apilim were killed by gunfire: Aharon Dov, Pinchas Weiss and Menachem Samet."

Source — USHMM Catalog pa1123714 (https://collections.ushmm.org/search/catalog/pa1123714):
> "Jewish DPs on board the illegal immigrant ship, the Theodor Herzl. April 14, 1947... Pnina Klein (née Halpern) and her brother Meir... were among the passengers on this vessel... Denied entry into Palestine by the British, the Halperns were sent along with the rest of the passengers to detention camps in Cyprus."

Source — Wikipedia Alicia Appleman-Jurman (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alicia_Appleman-Jurman):
> "In early 1947 she sailed aboard the Theodor Herzl, which was stopped by Britain's Royal Navy. The ship's crew and passengers were sent to Cyprus and interned for eight months there."

**Named on Theodor Herzl April 1947**:
- Pnina Halpern (later Klein), Meir Halpern, Leah Halpern
- Alicia Appleman-Jurman (then Alicia Jurman) of Buczacz
- Killed: Aharon Dov, Pinchas Weiss, Menachem Samet

**Correction**: an earlier dossier might have confused William Bernstein (killed on SS Exodus) with the Herzl. Bernstein was on the Exodus, NOT the Herzl. The 3 confirmed Herzl dead are Aharon Dov, Pinchas Weiss, Menachem Samet only.

**No online passenger list exists** — David, Lusia, Shimon are not on any public manifest. The 2,641 names are at: Haganah Archive (Tel Aviv) Mossad LeAliyah Bet ship files, or Central Zionist Archives Jerusalem under "Mossad LeAliyah Bet — Theodor Herzl 1947".

## JDC Cyprus name index — the most actionable database for finding our family

Source — JDC Archives Cyprus topic guide (https://archives.jdc.org/topic-guides/detained-in-cyprus/):
