# Yizkor books + photos — findings (2026-05-21)

## 🎯 THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT FIND OF THIS RESEARCH

The **1957 Bolechów Yizkor book** ("Sefer ha-zikaron le-kedoshei Bolechow", Y. Eshel editor) names the Rapaport-Weitzner marriage *directly*:

> **"D. Rapaport, who was Eli Weitzner's son-in-law, helped the Revisionist movement grow in Bolechow. The Revisionist ranks were organized with adults in 'Hatza'r', young workers and students in 'Massadah', and the youngest in 'Betar'."**

Source page: Yiddish 278-288 of the Bolechów Yizkor
URL: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/Bolekhov/bol278.html

This is **documentary, published-in-1957 confirmation**:
1. David Rapaport ("D. Rapaport") married into the Weitzner family
2. The Weitzner patriarch was **Eli** (= Eliyahu / Elias) Weitzner
3. David was active in the **Revisionist Zionist** movement in Bolechów (the family tradition has been that he was Betar in Nadwórna; the Yizkor places him also in Bolechów as a Revisionist organizer)

**KEY PASSAGES for our family**:

### Betar / Revisionist context
> "The first Beitar hachshara (training farm) in Poland was established in Nadwórna in 1929."

Source: Yad Vashem, https://www.yadvashem.org/yv/en/exhibitions/valley/nadworna/before_holocaust.asp

⇒ David Rapaport b.1911-12-25 would have been 17-18 years old when the first Polish Beitar training farm opened *in his own home town*. He was almost certainly a founding member.

JewishGen page nad041.html lists Nadwórna's Zionist youth groups: "Hashomer, Halzair, Gordonia, Frajhait, Ichud, Hapoel Mizrachi, **Betar**"

### Timber/forestry industry — David's later workplace context
From Pinkas Hakehillot Polin (Nadvirna entry): https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/pinkas_poland/pol2_00328.html

> "A company called **Foresta** was involved in tree-lumber cutting in the area for the Polish government and their preparation for export, with the operators and managers of this company being Jewish. However, because of the intervention of anti-Semitic organizations, both Polish and Ukrainian, the contract with the company was not renewed in 1937 and the Jews were laid off from their jobs."

> "In the early twenties a woodworking plant called **Zetperol** under Jewish ownership was built in Nadwórna and it supplied timber wood for packing crates for exported goods, with many Jewish families finding employment in this plant."

⇒ Foresta and Zetperol are likely candidates for David Rapaport's pre-war forestry employer and the sawmill he worked at under German occupation (1941-42).

## BOLECHÓW YIZKOR

**Book**: "Sefer ha-zikaron le-kedoshei Bolechow", Y. Eshel ed., 1957
- USHMM catalog: https://collections.ushmm.org/search/catalog/bib252715

- Index PDF (confirms Weitzner on pages 144 and 185): https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/indices/Bolekhiv_index.pdf

### Direct Rapaport-Weitzner family hit (the headline find)

**Yiddish page 278**: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/Bolekhov/bol278.html
> "D. Rapaport, who was **Eli Weitzner's son-in-law**, helped the Revisionist movement grow in Bolechow."

### Second Weitzner reference — Shlomele

**Bolechow Yizkor page bol086.html** (Hebrew 86-100 / Yiddish 264-284)
> "A youth by the name of **Shlomele Weitzner** gave much of his free time to the organization but unfortunately became ill with dysentery and died young."

⇒ Shlomele is plausibly a younger brother or cousin of Lusia/Leah/Feige/Moses/Pnina — same Weitzner family. This adds another sibling to investigate.

### Bolechów industry context — fits Eli Weitzner's career arc

**Bolechow Yizkor, chapter "Bolechów and its Jewish Population under Soviet Rule"**: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/bolekhov/bol117.html
- Includes Michael Schevis "Tanneries in Bolekhov" (p.101)
- Includes Abraham Weber "Wood and Other Industries" (p.102)

Direct quotes from the Yizkor:
> "Tanneries existed in Bolechow from the beginning of the 19th century as a cottage industry, and the first big leather factory was founded by Yisroel-Leyb Hauptman, who was also one of the first Jewish mayors."
> "There was a special minyon of the tannery workers in which an old preparer, Reb Yankl Schnee, taught the tanners."
> "Industrial undertakings established by Jews in Bolechow included **timber and other mills, tanneries, and furniture, soap, and candle factories**."
> "Jewish occupations in Bolechow in the 18th century included trade in Hungarian wines, **cattle**, horses, and salt from the local mines"

⇒ This matches Eli Weitzner's career arc precisely: cattle dealer → tannery owner.

### Name variant clarification

**"Bolechów Ruski" is the SAME town as Bolechów — not a separate village.**
Source: https://kehilalinks.jewishgen.org/bolekhov/res_sum.html — "Variant shtetl names: Bolechov, Bolechow, Bolechow Ruski"

## EDWARD GELLES — "Griffel of Nadwórna" — academic genealogy

Edward Gelles, an Oxford-archived genealogist, has documented the Griffel dynasty in depth. PDFs:

- http://archives.balliol.ox.ac.uk/Modern%20Papers/gelles/Griffel%20of%20Nadworna.pdf
- https://archives.balliol.ox.ac.uk/Modern%20Papers/gelles/2017PragueBrody.pdf
- https://archives.balliol.ox.ac.uk/Modern%20Papers/gelles/Facets%20of%20my%20Family%20History.%20Part%202.pdf

### Key established facts about Griffel-Nadwórna

> "**Eliezer Griffel (1850-1918) of Nadworna** and his wife Sarah Matel Chajes had ten children. Their two eldest sons, **David Mendel** and Isaac Chaim took over most of the Griffel enterprises."

> "Eliezer Griffel built up a business empire in timber and oil which gave employment to many members of his clan and to his townsfolk."

### CRITICAL NOTE — TWO Regina Griffels

- **Edward Gelles's published Regina Griffel** was b.1900 and married Dr David Isaac Gelles of Vienna. That is a different Regina from ours.
- **Our Rebeka/Regina Griffel** was b.1888 Nadwórna and married Berisz Rapaport.
- Both descend from the same Eliezer Griffel (1850-1918) — 10-child timber/oil patriarch.
- The two Reginas are **likely first or second cousins** within the extended Griffel clan.

### Implication for our family tree

The Griffel-Nadwórna PDF needs to be read end-to-end to place our Rebeka b.1888 in Eliezer's tree. If she is a granddaughter of Eliezer (rather than a cousin), the Rapaport family inherits direct genealogical links to the **Chajes, Wahl, and Gelles** rabbinical/scholarly dynasties of Galicia.
