## 1. The Hormak family of Nadwórna — Righteous nomination opportunity

**Status:** NOT yet recognised. Confirmed absent from both Yad Vashem Righteous Among the Nations and Polscy Sprawiedliwi (Polish Righteous) databases.

**The Hormaks' three documented acts of rescue** (per memoir Chapter D pp. 30, 32-33, 36):
1. Refused Lusia's last coat as payment for food; said *"take food and go in peace"*
2. Smuggled 4-year-old Shimon from Nadwórna to Lwów; a Hormak brother rode the train with Lusia for protection
3. Retrieved Shimon from the Lwów ghetto when execution rumours spread; brought him to Lusia at Legionów 24

**The Rapaport family CAN submit a nomination.** Yad Vashem accepts posthumous nominations from survivors OR their descendants.

**What you'd need to submit:**
- Notarised testimony from Shimon Rapaport (if living; would be ~89 in 2026), OR from descendants summarising the rescue
- The Lusia memoir chapter (Chapter D pp. 30, 32-33, 36) — primary source documenting all three rescue acts
- Any wartime photos or post-war correspondence between the Rapaport and Hormak families
- Corroborating testimonies from other Nadwórna survivors who knew of the rescue

**How:** https://www.yadvashem.org/righteous/how-to-apply.html — Department of the Righteous Among the Nations, Yad Vashem, Jerusalem.

## 2. Lota Rapaport — no record anywhere, this archive is her sole memorial

**Status:** No Page of Testimony was ever filed for Lota Rapaport. Yad Vashem, USHMM, JewishGen, Bełżec memorial, Lwów ghetto registers — all empty. David Memek (her brother) and Shimon (the nephew she sheltered) both lived long enough to file POTs for murdered family members and didn't file one for her.

**Most plausible fate, given the Lwów ghetto timeline:**
- August 1942: "Große Aktion" — 40,000-50,000 Lwów Jews rounded up at Janowska, deported to Bełżec. Statistically the single most likely event to have claimed her.
- November 1942: ~5,000 deported to Bełżec
- 5-7 January 1943: 15,000-20,000 shot outside the city; ghetto downgraded to *Judenlager Lemberg*
- 1-20 June 1943: final liquidation — survivors shot in the ghetto or sent to Janowska (by then a killing site)

**She refused Lusia's offer of shelter outside the ghetto** (memoir p. 38):
> *"Lucia, listen, you, who look like a Polish woman, can be outside the ghetto, but not I. Besides, I would have to, since I look very Jewish, hide in the basement all the time and eventually you will be caught because of me."*

**Narrative-ready conclusion for the LLM:** *The Hormak family carried Shimon out; Lota stayed. She has no grave and, until this archive, no written memorial — only Lusia's account preserves her.*

## 3. Shimon Rapaport — no first-person testimony in any public archive (+ Dakar correction)

**Status:** Zero matches across Yad Vashem video/written testimonies (O.3/O.33 series), USC Shoah Foundation VHA, Fortunoff Video Archive (Yale), USHMM, NLI JPress newspaper archive, and Hidden Child Foundation networks for a hidden-child survivor named Shimon/Szymon Rapaport born ~1937 in Nadwórna and hidden in Lwów.

The crate-at-Legionów-24 story currently survives ONLY in his mother Lusia's voice, not his own. Any first-person account Shimon gave is held privately by his children Hadas and Ronan, not in any public catalogue.

**IMPORTANT — the Dakar submarine story needs verification.** Family memoir says Shimon was assigned to sail on the submarine *INS Dakar* as a *Bamachane* reporter, was replaced at the last moment, and the Dakar sank (25 January 1968) with all 69 crew lost.

Direct verification finds: **the journalist who actually disembarked at Gibraltar and was saved was Eran Shorer of Kol Israel radio, not a *Bamachane* reporter.** Eran Shorer later published a book including his recordings from the voyage. The Shimon-Dakar story is therefore best treated as **unverified family memory** pending corroboration. Possible explanations:
- (a) Shimon was *considered* and then not assigned, with the actual selected journalist being Shorer — and the story compressed in retelling
- (b) A separate *Bamachane* near-miss was conflated with the Dakar story over time
- (c) Documentary evidence (a *Bamachane* assignment slip from January 1968) is held by Hadas/Ronan

**Action items for the archive:**
1. **Ask Hadas and Ronan directly** — does any Shimon-authored memoir/diary/recording exist? Was Shimon ever interviewed by Centropa, Massuah, Beit Lohamei Haghetaot, or any oral-history project? Any Bamachane assignment slip / Dakar paperwork preserved?
2. **Record a video interview WITH HADAS AND RONAN** about what Shimon told them — before more memory is lost.
3. **Email Yad Vashem Archives Reference** (`references.archives@yadvashem.org`) requesting any O.3/O.33 file under "Rapaport, Shimon b.1937 Nadwórna."
4. **Contact NLI JPress reading room** (`jpress@nli.org.il`) for in-system Hebrew search of `שמעון רפפורט` bylines, Tiberias and Haifa bureaus, 1958-1995.
5. **Submit Pages of Testimony** for the murdered Nadwórna Rapaports under Shimon's name (or his children's name) if not already filed — this would itself be an act of remembrance for Lota.
