## 2. Lusia's birth date — CONFIRMED 8 April 1913 Bolechów

**Answer:** Lusia (Łucja / Leah Rapaport née Weitzner) was born **8 April 1913 in Bolechów**.

**Decisive evidence:** death-age arithmetic. She died 28 December 1996 reported aged 83 → only a 1913 birth year is internally consistent. A 1916 birth would have made her age 80 in December 1996, not 83.

**Variants explained:**
- **6 April 1916** and **8 April 1916** — trace to the "Maria Cizlik" Aryan-side Kennkarte that survived as the de-facto record on early post-war DP and Polish exit papers. This is a documented Yad Vashem "Surviving Under False Identity" pattern. The 6↔8 split is a typical Polish clerk drift (6.IV vs 8.IV).
- **circa 1917** (1965 Yad Vashem submitter field) — Pages of Testimony submitter dates are notoriously approximate; weakest variant.

**Sources:**
- Yad Vashem "Surviving Under False Identity" exhibition — https://wwv.yadvashem.org/yv/en/exhibitions/false-identity/index.asp
- AGAD Polish Central Archives Jewish vital records (cuts off ~1900 — Bolechów 1913-17 records not in JRI-Poland / Gesher Galicia)
- Holocaust in Bolekhiv (Wikipedia) — only 48 Bolechów Jewish survivors known

## 3. Lusia + 4-year-old Shimon survival 1941-1944 — Shimon hidden SEPARATELY

**Answer:** Shimon (age 4-7 during the occupation) was most plausibly hidden **separately** from Lusia, NOT at Legionów 24.

**Reasoning:** A circumcised Jewish boy of that age could not credibly live full-time with a single Polish woman on the Aryan side. Yad Vashem and USHMM are explicit: circumcision was the single greatest obstacle for boys.

**The documented Lwów pattern** (per Joanna Beata Michlic's research on hidden children):
- Placement in a Catholic convent — Felician, Franciscan, or Albertine sisters all sheltered Jewish children in Lwów
- OR with a paid Polish family on Żegota's payroll (Lwów branch documented)
- Mother visited under her Aryan identity
- Reunion typically came post-July 1944 Soviet liberation of Lwów

**Sources:**

## 4. Why the 1945-46 paper separation (Lusia in Polish records, David in Brussels DP) — Bricha reunion pattern

**Answer:** Lusia and Shimon left Lwów after the July 1944 Soviet liberation, repatriated to Poland under the 1944-46 transfers, where Lusia registered with **CKŻP as "Rapaport née Weitzner"** (real name, re-Judaised post-liberation). From spring 1946 they joined the Bricha through Czechoslovakia, reaching Brussels by mid-1946 — early enough for Dov's conception/birth in November 1946.

David likely reached Brussels first via the same Bricha route — a typical "men first" pattern (men crossed first to secure papers and housing). In Brussels JDC/HIAS files Lusia would appear under "Rapaport" — the Cizlik identity discarded once safe.
