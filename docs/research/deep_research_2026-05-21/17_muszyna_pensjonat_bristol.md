# Muszyna — the hotel Lusia managed identified (2026-05-21)

## 🎯 The hotel Lusia managed in Muszyna = Pensjonat "Bristol"

Most likely identification: **Pensjonat "Bristol"** owned by the **Weiss family** (Chaim Weiss).

Source: Wirtualny Sztetl (Polin Museum), Muszyna community history page (CC-licensed):
https://sztetl.org.pl/en/towns/m/1209-muszyna/99-history/137715-history-of-community

Key sourced facts (paraphrased):
- Pre-1929 Muszyna had only two hotels: one near the railway station owned by Chaim Weiss; one on the Market Square owned by Haskel Reich
- In the 1930s, several Jewish-owned boarding houses appeared — including the **famous "Bristol"** owned by the Weiss family — known for drawing well-off Jewish patients and holidaymakers
- Muszyna became an officially-recognised spa town in 1929-30 after mineral-spring development by mayor Antoni Jurczak and doctor Seweryn Mściwujewski

The memoir says Lusia *rented* the hotel — which matches the typical pattern of a Weiss-owned building leased seasonally to an outside operator-manager. Lusia, from a Bolechów Jewish family with the means to lease and the social skills to manage a fine resort hotel, fits the profile of such a lessee.

## Dedicated published history exists

A standalone 6-page article was published in **Almanach Muszyny 1998** (the town's official annual history journal):

R. Żebrowski, "Żydowski pensjonat 'Bristol' w Muszynie" ("The Jewish 'Bristol' Pensjonat in Muszyna"), *Almanach Muszyny* 1998, pp. 27-33.

URL: http://www.almanachmuszyny.pl/spisy/1998/AM1998_05_zydowski_pensjonat_bristol_w_muszynie.pdf

⇒ The fact that the Bristol got a dedicated standalone article 60 years after the war is strong evidence it was the dominant pre-war Jewish-resort establishment in Muszyna — exactly the kind of "fine hotel" that drew the memoir's "cream and social élite of Moszyna".

## Holocaust fate of Muszyna Jews — matches memoir escape narrative

Per Sztetl (paraphrased):
- September 1939: German troops took Muszyna (~800 Jewish residents)
- November 1940: Jews ordered to leave
- December 1940: Jewish population liquidated → deported to Grybów + Bobowa → Nowy Sącz ghetto → ultimately Bełżec

The memoir says David, Lusia, and Shimon fled east toward Soviet-occupied territory **before** these deportations — consistent with the standard "fled when Germans arrived in September 1939" pattern.
