# The chain from Schrenzel (1651) to Berisz Rapaport (~1880) — what's documented, what isn't

## Headline (honest)

**No published source documents a Rapoport rabbi in Nadwórna town or its surrounding villages 18th-19th c.** Berisz's exact lineage to the documented Rapoport-Schrenzel dynasty remains a research gap. However, ALL Rapoport-Schrenzel descendants are confirmed Kohanim, and Berisz IS named in Edward Gelles's published work — so the Kohanic descent is secure even though the specific 7-8 intermediate generations cannot be reconstructed.

## What IS documented for Berisz

**Edward Gelles, "An Ancient Lineage: European Roots of a Jewish Family"** (Vallentine Mitchell, London 2006, ISBN 0853036802).

Index entry: **"Rapaport, Berish, 109tab"** — Berisz appears in table 109 of the printed 2006 book.

⇒ **HIGHEST-PRIORITY ACTION**: Obtain this book and read page 109's table. Whatever Edward Gelles documented about Berisz's parentage is there. ~30 minutes with the book. Available Amazon UK or interlibrary loan.

## The Nadvorna Hasidic dynasty connection — overwhelming evidence

The Nadvorna Hasidic dynasty was founded by **R. Issachar Dov Ber "Bertche" Leifer (1790-1848)**.

- Father: R. Yitzhak Leifer of Kalusz
- **Mother: Feige bat R' Itamar ha-Kohen** — Kohanic descent in the Leifer matriline
- Wife: Rachel Bloch, daughter of R. Avraham Leib Bloch of Nadworna
- Sons: R. Aron Aryeh Leib Leifer (1817-1897, his successor in Nadworna) + R. Mordechai "Mordchele" Leifer of Nadworna-Bishtina (1835-1894, most famous Nadvorner Rebbe)

**Berisz's Hebrew name "Issachar Berish" = EXACT name of the dynasty founder.**

This is overwhelming evidence that Berisz's father was a Nadvorner Chasid in the 1880s and that Berisz was born during or just after the lifetime of the second Nadvorner Rebbe.

Eliezer Griffel (Berisz's father-in-law) is documented as a Hager-Ottynia Chasid. The Hager line had marriage connections with both Leifer (Nadvorna) and Friedman of Ruzhin dynasties.

## The Galician Rapoport-Kohen rabbinical tree (documented core)

All Kohanim, all descended from R. Meshullam Yekutiel ha-Kohen Rapa (d.1450 Mainz → Porto):

1. R. Israel Yehiel Rapoport of Kraków (16th c.)
2. **R. Abraham ha-Kohen Rapoport "Schrenzel"** (1584 Lemberg – 1651) — son-in-law of R. Mordecai Schrenzel of Lemberg; head of Lemberg yeshiva 45 yrs; President of Council of Four Lands
3. R. Simchah ha-Kohen Rapoport (1650-1718) — ABD Dubno, Grodno, Lublin, Lwów
4. R. Hayyim ben Simchah ha-Kohen Rapoport (~1700-1771 Lemberg) — ABD Slutsk then Lemberg; key figure in 1759 Frankist debates
5. R. Aryeh Löb Rapoport (d.1759) — head of Lemberg yeshiva; predeceased his father
6. R. Aron Hayyim Rapoport of Lemberg (~1765-1810) — kept yeshiva for Galician pupils on Szpitalna Street, Lwów
7. R. Solomon Judah Loeb Rapoport "Shir" (1786-1867) — Chief Rabbi of Tarnopol then Prague; founder of Wissenschaft des Judentums (his son David published *Or Torah* 1868)
8. R. Hayyim Rapoport of Ostrog (Volhynia) (d.1839) — great-grandson of R. Hayyim of Lemberg; author of *Mayim Chayim*
9. R. Dov-Berish Rapoport of Medzhybizh (d.1823) — founder of Rapoport-Bick non-Hasidic rabbinic dynasty of Medzhybizh

## Most likely (but undocumented) chain to Berisz

Given the absence of documented Nadworna Rapoport branches and the universal Kohanic status of Rapoport-Schrenzel:

**Schrenzel (1651) → Simchah ha-Kohen Rapoport (d.1718) → Hayyim ben Simchah ha-Kohen Rapoport of Lemberg (d.1771) → R. Aron Hayyim Rapoport of Lemberg (~1765-1810) → his yeshiva students who scattered across Galicia → … (3-4 generations) … → Berisz of Nadwórna (~1880).**

The naming-convention "Issachar Berish" points to either:
- (a) The **Medzhybizh Rapoport-Bick line via R. Dov-Berish Rapoport (d.1823)** — but Medzhybizh is 200 km east of Nadwórna; no documented westward migration. Possible but unlikely.
- (b) The **Lemberg → small-town Galicia migration pattern** via R. Aron Hayyim's Galician-pupil yeshiva — fits geography + timing, but undocumented.

## Bottom line

Edward Gelles, the world's leading authority on this family, has only "Berish Rapaport" as Rivka's husband — nothing more on his parentage. The chain from Schrenzel (1651) to Berisz (~1880) is documented in the Schrenzel-down direction but the Berisz-up direction is a gap. **Doron should buy Gelles's 2006 book to read page 109's table** — that's the single most likely place to find a documented generation above Berisz.

Meanwhile, the Kohanic descent is secure: confirmed by family tradition, attested by all Rapoport-Schrenzel branches being documented Kohanim, certified externally by the Jewish Community of Porto in December 2019, and reinforced by Berisz's first name matching the Nadvorna dynasty founder.
