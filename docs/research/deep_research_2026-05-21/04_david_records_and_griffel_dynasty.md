## DAVID MEMEK RAPAPORT — record trail

### Confirmed wartime context (not yet a personal record, but the documentary universe)

- **Pre-war Nadwórna timber industry**: Jewish-owned. The Foresta company (leased Polish state woodlands, all-Jewish workforce) was cancelled in 1937 under antisemitic pressure. The Zetperol woodworking plant (early 1920s) employed many Jewish families.
- **Wartime sawmill**: After the October 1941 mass aktion, "Some 600 Jews worked daily at the saw mill" in Nadwórna (Yad Vashem). The sawmill was under German-appointed Treuhänder management. The most likely large timber operator with the scale to be Treuhänder was the **Glesinger family** (international timber industrialists with Galicia operations).
- **Ghetto liquidation 24 October 1942**: "All the Jews were taken to the synagogue and after they had been robbed of all their valuables some of them were taken to Stanisławów and murdered there, the others were killed on the spot." (Pinkas Hakehillot, https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/pinkas_poland/pol2_00328.html)
- The wood-train escape (18 men escaped via wood-transport wagons that David had drilled escape-holes in) — the **Nadwórna Yizkor book** (Carmi/Kramer 1975) is the single most likely source for a named, on-the-record account: https://www.yiddishbookcenter.org/collections/yizkor-books/yzk-nybc313901/carmi-israel-nadvurnah-sefer-edut-ve-zikaron

### Theodor Herzl ship — 2 April 1947, Sète → 13 April 1947 interception

- Sailed Sète, **2 April 1947** with **2,641 passengers** drawn from "two camps in France and Belgium"
- Intercepted 13 April 1947 by HMS Haydon and HMS St Brides Bay
- Three killed, 27 wounded
- Deported to Cyprus camps (Karaolos near Famagusta; Nicosia; Dhekelia; Xylotymbou)
- Palmach/Palyam history PDF: https://www.palyam.org/English/Hahapala/hf/hf_Theodor_Herzl.pdf

David, Shimon, and Lusia almost certainly boarded at Sète after a Belgian DP-camp road convoy.

**Wertheimer Haapalah passenger pages** have a "Weitzner, Lea" entry that needs to be verified — could be Lusia. https://www.wertheimer.info/family/GRAMPS/Haapalah/
