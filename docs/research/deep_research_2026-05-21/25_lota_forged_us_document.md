# Dossier 25 — Lota's forged US-resident document + her unnamed husband (2026-05-21)

A focused research thread opened when re-reading memoir Chapter D page 41 revealed details missing from earlier passes:

> *"Poles with forged documents, certifying that the holder was a resident of the United States... Each such forged document cost ten thousand dollars. Wealthy Jews of the ghetto tried to save their lives by purchasing these documents. **Lota also bought such a document, but the sellers informed on the transaction to the Gestapo, and Lota AND HER HUSBAND were taken to a place from which they never returned.**"*

Two new things this reveals:

1. **Lota's specific cause of death**: she and her husband were arrested by the Gestapo after being denounced by the sellers of a forged US-residency certificate. NOT a generic ghetto liquidation; a specific betrayal.
2. **Lota had a husband.** His name is not in the memoir. He was a Rapaport-by-marriage. He died with her. He vanishes from history if not recorded here.

## Do we have Lota's actual card?

**No.** It was the very instrument that got her killed — the Gestapo would have confiscated it as evidence of the "crime" of false citizenship and destroyed it (or filed it in Gestapo trial papers that themselves did not survive the war). Direct searches of USHMM, Yad Vashem, EHRI returned no Lwów-specific surviving example of this document type.

## The scheme — identified

The Lwów forged-US-resident trade was a regional variant of the broader 1942-43 fake-foreign-citizenship market across occupied Poland. The scheme had **two modalities**:

### (a) Gestapo-run central traps

Best documented: **Hotel Polski, Warsaw, May–July 1943.** German agents and Jewish collaborators spread word that holders of neutral-country passports (Paraguay, Honduras, El Salvador, Peru, Chile) would be exchanged for Reich citizens interned abroad. ~3,000 Jews lured out of hiding were deported to Bergen-Belsen → Vittel → mostly Auschwitz. References:
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hotel_Polski
- https://www.jhi.pl/en/articles/the-70th-anniversary-of-the-liquidation-of-hotel-polski,22

### (b) Free-lance profiteer-betrayer scams

Polish/Ukrainian profiteers sold forged or stolen documents in ghettos including Lwów, then sometimes denounced their buyers to the Gestapo for a second payment. **Lusia's account of Lota matches modality (b)** — the sellers informed *after* the sale, not before; this is a free-lance profiteer pattern, not a centrally-run Gestapo trap.

## The $10,000 figure — plausibility

Scholarly sources on Bernese Group passports cite **CHF 500–2,000 (~$120–500 USD 1943)** as the *production* cost — see https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/jewish-history_a-tale-of-passports--profiteers-and-escaping-nazi-persecution/45479902. But **street prices** to desperate ghetto buyers ran an order of magnitude higher. Hotel Polski documents reportedly sold for tens of thousands of zlotys. **$10,000 for a US document with intent-to-denounce is high but not implausible** — the price could be extracted because the seller knew delivery was moot.

## Surviving comparable documents — visual proxies for the narrator

While no Lwów US-resident certificate is known to survive, the closest visual analogue is the **Eiss Archive** — ~15 forged Paraguayan passports of the **Ładoś ("Bernese") Group** operation. Now held at:

- **Auschwitz-Birkenau State Museum** (acquired 2019, second tranche 2020): https://www.auschwitz.org/en/museum/news/the-auschwitz-museum-acquires-the-second-part-of-the-archive-concerning-the-lados-group,1414.html
- **Pilecki Institute** (exhibition): https://instytutpileckiego.pl/en/wystawy/relacje-z-wystaw/paszporty-wystawa-o-grupie-ladosia
- **Passports for Life** digitisation: https://passportsforlife.pl/dokumenty/

These are **embeddable as research context** in the archive — clearly labelled as a parallel scheme, not Lota's actual paper. They give the LLM narrator a concrete image to invoke when describing the document type that killed Lota.

## Lota's husband — open thread

No "Rapaport" Lwów ghetto registration matching Lota + spouse surfaced in indexed searches. Direct queries to **Yad Vashem Hall of Names** (https://yvng.yadvashem.org/) and **JewishGen Lwów** (https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/lviv/) are the next step — flag as an open research thread.

A single hit at Yad Vashem Names DB for "Rapaport" + place "Lwów" + spouse "Lota" would name him. Worth a ~5-minute browser search.

## For the LLM narrator

When telling Lota's story, the narrator can now say:

> She bought a document — a forged certificate that she was a resident of the United States. It cost ten thousand dollars, an enormous sum. The sellers were Polish profiteers. They took her money and then they took her name to the Gestapo and asked for more money for that. Her husband, whose name was not preserved, was arrested with her. They were taken to a place from which they never returned. The document — the one thing that might have saved them — was the very thing that killed them.

That's not just genealogy. That's a story about how survival was sold, and how trust killed.
