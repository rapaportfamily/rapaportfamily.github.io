# Legionów 24, Lwów = Prospekt Svobody 24, Lviv — building identified (2026-05-21)

## Identification

Lusia's wartime apartment at **ulica Legionów 24** in 1943-1944 Lwów is **Prospekt Svobody 24** in today's Lviv. The street numbering on the Legionów (west) side of the boulevard was preserved through Polish → Soviet → Ukrainian renaming periods.

## The building

- **Address today**: Prospekt Svobody 24, Lviv, Ukraine
- **Built**: 1836-1837
- **Architect**: Johann Salzmann
- **Style**: Biedermeier (originally three storeys; a fourth was added later)
- **Materials**: stone kamienica
- **Original commissioner**: F. Adamski
- **Famous earlier resident**: Wincenty Pol (Polish national poet, father of Polish geography) — lived here 1856-1866
- **Pre-war use** (interwar period): ground floor housed a confectionery shop; upper floors were rented apartments — exactly the kind of building where a Polish-Catholic-passing Jew with forged papers could live unnoticed

## Street-name history

- Pre-1919: Karola Ludwika Street (Habsburg-era)
- 1919-1940: **ulica Legionów** (in honour of Józef Piłsudski's Polish Legions of WWI) — this is the name Lusia uses in the memoir
- 1940-41 (first Soviet occupation): Pershoho travnia ("First of May") Street
- 1941-44 (Nazi occupation): **Adolf-Hitler-Ring** (Lemberg's main administrative boulevard) — divided at first into Opernstrasse + Museumstrasse
- 1944-91 (Soviet): Lenina Boulevard
- 1991-today: **Prospekt Svobody** (Liberty Avenue) — Lviv's central boulevard

Source: Lviv Interactive "Adolf Hitler Ring" — https://streets.lvivcenter.org/en/Adolf-Hitler-Ring/

## The German military base "opposite"

The memoir says a German military base directly opposite #24 fired gunshots frequently. The Adolf-Hitler-Ring was the principal Nazi administrative boulevard of Lemberg 1941-44, hosting Standort-Kommandantur, Stadtkommandantur, Wehrmacht-Nachrichten-Kommandantur, Transport-Kommandantur. The Lviv Opera House (350m up at today's Svobody 28) functioned as Wehrmacht entertainment HQ ("Operntheater Lemberg").

The most likely specific "base opposite #24" would be a Hetmańska-side building directly across the central tree-lined promenade. The promenade is only ~50m wide — well within rifle range. Identifying the exact building requires the 1942-43 *Amtsblatt für den Distrikt Galizien* or Bundesarchiv Lemberg-stationing records.
