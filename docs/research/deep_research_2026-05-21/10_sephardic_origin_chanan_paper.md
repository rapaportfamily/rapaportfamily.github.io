### C) The 1305 Dr Vidal Rapapa rescue conspiracy

Documented in Antonio Pons, *Los Judíos del Reino de Mallorca durante los siglos XIII y XIV* (v.2 p.29):

A young Jewish woman planned to convert to Christianity to marry her Christian lover. A group of Mallorcan Jews — **Dr Vidal Rapapa** (group leader), Aahron (in whose house the girl was hidden), Maimon ben Estruc Ibn-Nunu, David ben Sopran, and Ensrum of Suria (Catalonia) — kidnapped her and hid her. Spanish Church investigators uncovered the conspirators and freed the girl. The group was sentenced to long imprisonment + heavy fines. After a large bribe, King Jaime II reduced the sentences in exchange for Dr Vidal Rapapa's pledge to keep the matter secret.

This proves Rapapa is unambiguously a Jewish name (a non-Jewish group would not have endangered itself to prevent a Jewish girl's conversion).

### D) The 1345 Dr Jucef Salomon Rapapa lawsuit

Court physician (Physicus) to King Jaime III of Mallorca. In 1345 filed suit against successor King Pedro IV for non-payment of 10 Libres for medical services rendered to Jaime III and his entourage. Dr Chanan Rapaport's analysis (consulting Prof. Jeff Malka, citing artist Bonaventura Perpinya's 1309 contract paying 4 Libres per major artistic work for King Sancho I) establishes that **1 Libra in 1345 ≈ €60,000-€150,000 in modern purchasing power** — so 10 Libres ≈ €600,000-€1.5 million. A respectable physician's fee.

### E) Etymology — RAP A la PortA = "predator at the gate"

The Spanish/Catalan word RAPace and the Latin RAPax both mean "a predator". A reasonable choice for families of strength, wealth, power, and influence who added powerful symbols to their banners, heraldry, and gates. A well-known parallel: the Lione = Lion family affixed a lion to their gate and called themselves "Lione de la Porta".

For the Rapapa family: **RAP A la Porta** → RAPAPA = "A predator at the gate". The German RABe = Raven (predator bird) which in daily slang is also RAPe — same predator bird.

### F) The transition Spain → Italy → name change to Rapa-Porto

After the 1391 pogroms and the 1492 expulsion, the family fled to Mainz (Germany), then was expelled again from Mainz, then to Nuremberg, then to Italy via the Adige river ports: Legnago, Mestre, Venice, **Portobuffolè** (45°48'N / 12°19'E on the Livaza river), and Piove di Sacco.

**Isaac 'HaMoel' ben Yechiel Michael ha-Kohen Rapa** (father b.1502 Portobuffolè) was the first to add "Porto" to the surname ~1550. He referred to himself as "HaOrvi" (of the ravens), following the tradition of descriptive names ("HaGiladi", "HaShomroni", "HaGlili").
