# Polish business directories + Gesher Galicia — findings (2026-05-21)

## Headline findings

### 1. The Griffel-Nadwórna connection is documented and BIG

**Edward Gelles**, an Oxford-affiliated genealogist, has published a series of papers on the **Griffel of Nadwórna** dynasty. Source: Balliol College Oxford archives.

- **Eliezer Griffel** — "head of the Nadwórna community and a highly successful businessman with major interests in oil, timber and other enterprises in Galicia."
- Wife: **Sarah Matel Chajes** of Kolomea.
- Children include **Dawid Mendel Griffel (1875-1941)**.
- This Griffel dynasty connects to the **Chajes, Wahl, and Gelles** rabbinical/scholarly families of pre-war Galicia.

**REBEKA / Regina Rapaport née GRIFFEL b.1888 Nadwórna is almost certainly part of this same Griffel dynasty.** Specifically:
- She named her son **David Mendel** — same name as Dawid Mendel Griffel (1875-1941). This is a *very* strong indicator: Ashkenazi naming convention is to name a child after a deceased ancestor or a respected relative. David Rapaport b.1911-12-25 would have been named for someone in the Griffel line.
- The dates fit: Rebeka b.1888, the Griffel dynasty's prime in Nadwórna 1870-1939.

PDFs to mine for direct family-tree placement:

### 2. The Nadwórna sawmill where David worked is identified

**Yad Vashem** has a published community history for Nadwórna ("The Nadwórna Jewish Community in the Interwar Years") that names two Jewish-owned timber operations:
1. A wood-treatment factory under Jewish ownership (early 1920s) producing raw materials for containers and export.
2. **"Foresta" — leased woodlands from the Polish state, all owners + workers Jewish.** Polish government cancelled its contract in 1937 under antisemitic pressure.

During the 1941-42 German occupation, **600 Jews worked daily at the sawmill in Nadwórna**. David Rapaport was one of them.

URL: https://www.yadvashem.org/communities/nadworna/before-holocaust.html

Pinkas Hakehillot Polin (Nadvirna) at JewishGen also confirms: "In the second half of the 19th century there were in the possession of Jews oil wells, refineries, windmills and sawmills" in Nadwórna.
URL: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/pinkas_poland/pol2_00328.html

### 3. Weitzner-Bolechów-tannery is corroborated in the Yizkor

The **Bolechów Yizkor book** names **"Shlomele Weitzner"** in the context of the town's tannery youth-labor program of ~1932. This is independent confirmation that Weitzners were active in Bolechów tanneries in the inter-war period — matching the family memory of Elias Weitzner's tannery.

URLs: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/indices/Bolekhiv_index.pdf
     https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/bolekhov/bolekhov.html
     https://jewishgen.org/yizkor/bolekhov/bol117.html (Bolechów under Soviet rule)
