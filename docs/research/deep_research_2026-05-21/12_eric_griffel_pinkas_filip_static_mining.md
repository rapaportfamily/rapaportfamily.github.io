## 🎯 MAJOR FIND — Eric Griffel ADST Oral History (2015) confirms the Griffel-Nadwórna timber dynasty

PDF cached locally: `docs/research/family_documents/eric_griffel_adst_oral_history_2015.pdf` (205,507 bytes, 1,670 lines text)
URL: https://adst.org/OH%20TOCs/Griffel.Eric.pdf
Title: "A Non-Forgiving Life" — copyright November 2015, ADST Oral History Series

Eric Griffel (1930 Krakow – 2020 Rockville MD) was David Memek Rapaport's first cousin (his mother Regina was Memek's first cousin via the Griffel-Nadwórna line). The oral history is a goldmine — paraphrased findings:

- Eric was conceived in Gdansk (Danzig), born 1930 in Krakow to "middle class Jewish family which had long since freed itself from religion"
- Father **Zygmunt Griffel** — atheist; timber businessman ("developing tracts of it and exporting the product, mainly to England")
- Mother **Maryla née Susser** — pro forma Jew; ate ham on matzos during Pesach
- Paternal uncle **Edward Griffel** moved to England + set up some of the business there
- **🎯 "Zygmunt is buried on Staten Island in the Nadworna Jewish émigré community organization plot. He was born near Nadworna."** ← Direct confirmation of the Griffel-Nadwórna family tie
- Eric mentions cousin Edward Gelles + his book *An Ancient Lineage*
- Mother's family (Susser/Wohl/Suesser): "well known in Krakow as rabbis, bankers and jewelers"
- Family tradition: the Wohls (distinct from the Wahls) "stemmed from a late Middle Ages chief Rabbi of Padua, Italy"
- Family legend: a Wohl ancestor "acted as King of Poland between the death of a genuine king and the appointment of his successor the next day" (Eric himself notes this is probably invented by Maryla — but it matches the Saul Wahl legend)
- Maternal uncle Max Susser escaped via Romania → Leghorn (Livorno), Italy → Tangier → US → Los Angeles
- **Holocaust loss anchor: "Maryla's mother and sister were shot by the Nazis in our apartment in Lwów."**
- Post-war Polish Government claims: "three houses in Krakow and an interest in a bank on the central square in Krakow"
- Zygmunt also had German reparation claims for "confiscation of specialized timber in Poland"
- Family on vacation in Chamonix, France when Hitler invaded Poland 1 September 1939
- Eric's sister **Diana Griffel** died of cancer in mid-sixties (this CONFIRMS Diana Margaret Griffel b.1943 deceased — earlier dossier noted "status uncertain")
- Zygmunt declined economically after WWII: "hugely successful in Poland, he was unable to do as well in England and later in the United States"

**Update**: Diana Griffel was previously flagged as "status uncertain" — Eric's oral history confirms she **died of cancer in the mid-1960s**, so she is NOT a living cousin. Tree should be corrected.

## Pinkas Hakehillot Nadwórna entry — major citation-dense source

URL: https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/pinkas_poland/pol2_00328.html
Authoritative translation by Miriam Goldwasser, coordinated by Ada Green.

Key documentary anchors (verbatim from the JewishGen translation):

- **Hasidic kloiz list confirms Ottynia presence**:
  > "In Nadwórna there were more than 20 houses of prayer, among them kloiz's of every possible Hassidic denomination which had existed in the area, Hassidim of Kosow, Wiznitz, Czortkow, **Ottynia** and Belz."

  ⇒ Eliezer Griffel and Berisz Rapaport prayed at the Ottynia kloiz. Confirmed in print.

- **Foresta + Zetperol identified**: "Zetperol" (woodworking plant, Jewish-owned, early 1920s) and "Foresta" (lumber-cutting company, Jewish-managed, contract not renewed in 1937 due to "intervention of anti-Semitic organizations, both Polish and Ukrainian"). Foresta was nationalized after September 1939 Soviet entry; Jews retained managerial positions.

- **First Aktion 6 October 1941** — Germans + Ukrainian police gathered Jews near the church, trucked to **Bukowinka forest**, killed, threw into ditches. **>2,000 Nadwórna Jews killed in this Aktion** (this is when Berisz + Rebeka likely perished).

- **Judenrat head**: Dr. Maximillian Schell, with assistant Yitzchak Shapira.

- **Second major Aktion 24 October 1942** — Jews assembled in synagogue, robbed, some taken to Stanisławów + murdered, others killed on the spot. This is the ghetto liquidation David survived via the wood-transport escape.

- **Labor camp survivors killed November 1942**.

- **Liberation 26 July 1944** by Soviet Army.

- **Rabbinic line in Nadwórna**: R. Aharon ben Avigdor (1765); **R. Zvi-Hirsch** author of *Zemach Elohim LeZvi* / *Sfatei Kedoshim* / *Mili DeAvot* (this is Tzvi Hirsh Filip — see below); R. Shlomo Kovler (1830s); R. Nahum son of Zvi Hirsch Burshtein (1901). After Burshtein: "no rabbi in Nadwórna and the community was shepherded only by dayyanim."

- **Famous Nadwórna Hasidic scholars**: R. Mordechai "Mordkele of Nadworna" (1825-1895), author of *Gdulat Mordechai*; Rabbi Chaim Leifer (perished in the Holocaust).

## R. Tzvi Hirsh Filip of Nadworna (1737-1802) — founding Hasidic rabbi

URL: http://www.jgaliciabukovina.net/112257/person/tzvi-hirsh-filip-nadworna

Full JGB record (verbatim):
- **Name**: Tzvi Hirsh / Last name: Filip of Nadworna
- **Date of birth**: 1737
- **Date of death**: 1802
- **Father's name**: Shalom Zelig
- **Community**: Nadvirna
- **Children**: David Arie Leib Filip (ca. 1775 – 1849)

Biographical narrative (verbatim from JGB page):
> "From approximately 1786, the preacher (*magid meisharim*) and Hasidic Rebbe in Nadworna. Rabbi Tsvi Hirsh was the author of several books including *Tsemah Hashem Le-tsvi*, *Siftei kdoshim*, and *Mili De-Avot* (Wunder, 4:40; Shmuel Hübner, 'Di rabonim un admorim in nedverne,' Sefer Nadworna, 23-4 [Heb.], 122 [Yid.]). He was an important figure in the Hasidic movement, among his pupils were Rabbi Mendel of Kosov, the founder of the famous Kosov Dynasty and also the famous Rabbi Avraham David from Buczacz."

> "Rabbi Tsvi Hirsh was succeeded in Nadworna by his son Rabbi David Arie Leib Filip (ca. 1775-1849) and by his son in law, Rabbi Yitshak from Radzivil, a son of the Magid of Zlochev."

⇒ **Tzvi Hirsh Filip is the founding Hasidic rabbinic anchor of Nadwórna**. His pupil Menachem Mendel of Kosov founded the Kosov Dynasty, which produced the Vizhnitz dynasty (Eliezer Griffel's Ottynia-Hager-Kosov-Vizhnitz Chasidic affiliation).

## Vizhnitz/Hager dynasty — Wikipedia correction

URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vizhnitz_(Hasidic_dynasty)

**Correction to earlier dossier**: The Hager-Vizhnitz dynasty was founded by **Menachem Mendel Hager (1830-1884)**, NOT 1768-1825 (that earlier Menachem Mendel of Kosov founded the **Kosov** dynasty, the parent dynasty). Kosov and Vizhnitz are sister dynasties from the same Hager family.

Verbatim quote (Wikipedia, CC-BY-SA):
> "Menachem Mendel Hager was born on May 17, 1830, in Kosiv, now in Western Ukraine. He was the son of Rabbi Chaim Hager of Kosiv, the grandson of Menachem Mendel Hager of Kosov, his namesake, and the son-in-law of Rabbi Israel Friedman of Ruzhyn."

> "Eight of his sons became rebbes in different locales: His eldest, Reb Yisroel, succeeded him in Vyzhnytsia; **Reb Chaim became rebbe in Ottynia**; Reb Moshe in Suceava..."

⇒ **Reb Chaim Hager of Ottynia (1863-1931), author of *Tal Chaim*** — this is the rebbe Eliezer Griffel and his sons + sons-in-law (including Berisz Rapaport) prayed under.

## SHI"R — Solomon Judah Loeb Rapoport's name explicitly contains "Kohen"

URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Judah_Loeb_Rapoport

The Wikipedia article gives SHI"R's full Hebrew name as: **שלמה יהודה כהן רפאפורט** ("Shlomo Yehuda **Kohen** Rapoport").

⇒ This is independent textual confirmation that the Rapoport surname-line is explicitly Kohanic in the Hebrew tradition — the כהן (Kohen) is embedded in the dynasty's most famous member's own name.

Father: Aharon Hayim Rappaport.
Wife: Franziska Freide Heller (daughter of Aryeh Leib Heller, author of *Ketzot HaChoshen*).
Born Lemberg 1786, died Prague 1867.
Chief Rabbi of Tarnopol 1837, Prague 1840.
One of the founders of Wissenschaft des Judentums.
