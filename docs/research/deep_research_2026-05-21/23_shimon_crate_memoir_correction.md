# Dossier 23 — Shimon survived 1941-44 in a CRATE at Legionów 24 (memoir-corrected)

**Verified 2026-05-21 against the PRIMARY SOURCE** — Lusia's memoir, *Sipura shel Lusia* (ed. Moshe Guter), Chapter D, pages 30-46.

## The correction

T14/T16 inferred from secondary scholarship that Shimon Rapaport (b.1937, age 4-7 during the German occupation of Lwów) was most likely hidden in a Lwów Catholic convent or with a paid Polish family on Żegota's payroll. That inference was REASONABLE given the demographic facts (circumcised Jewish boys could not pass on the Aryan side full-time) but it was WRONG. The memoir tells the actual story.

## The actual story, in Lusia's own words

### Step 1 — Nadwórna ghetto established (autumn 1941). The Hormak family helped.

> *"Lusia had gentile friends, kind-hearted people from the Hormak family. This family was a dealer in Jewish property. ... In time of trouble, Hormak helped her transfer Simon to Lvov."* — page 30
>
> *"I am not returning to the ghetto and I have no intention whatsoever of living there,"* — Lusia to Memek, page 32
>
> *"Memek went to the ghetto and received a worker's tag. His essential work allowed him to leave the ghetto freely."* — page 32

The Hormak family — gentile Nadwórna neighbours who dealt in Jewish property and rewarded Lusia's earlier kindness — became the bridge. They smuggled Shimon out of Nadwórna and put him on a train to Lwów. Hormak's brother bought tickets and travelled WITH Lusia for protection (page 33).

### Step 2 — Lota hides Shimon in the Lwów ghetto

> *"All that time he lived with Lucia's sister-in-law, Lota, in the ghetto. All Jews were transferred to the ghetto and were forced to wear a yellow patch."* — page 38

Lota (David Memek's sister) sheltered Shimon inside the Lwów ghetto for the period before Lusia retrieved him. The Lwów ghetto was at that point "open" — Lusia could enter and commerce flourished between Poles and Jews (page 38).

### Step 3 — The retrieval

> *"The night was long. The clock seemed to stop ticking. Lucia thought only of Shimon: how is the little one feeling? What is in his heart? What are his thoughts? What does a small child separated from his parents feel? ... Hormek went to the ghetto to bring Shimon. The ghetto in Lvov was then open and one could leave it freely. Horror rumors circulated as if within a few days they would execute the children."* — page 36

Rumours that the Germans were about to execute the ghetto's children triggered the retrieval. Hormak again was the operator. Lusia spent the night with Hormak's sister in Lwów; morning brought Shimon back into her arms.

### Step 4 — The crate at Legionów 24

> *"Shimon and the Crate. Shimon lived with his mother and spent entire days alone in a crate whose dimensions were 70 cm × 1.50 m. The crate was his dwelling and his place of safety. In the morning, before leaving for work, Lusia would leave him food in the crate, and in the evening when ..."* — page 45
>
> *"From the outside, his complexion was white and pale, since he did not see sunlight for many long years. At night he slept with his mother. In the evening, when neighbors came to visit Lusia, she would operate the phonograph that was placed on the box and music would burst forth from it."* — page 46

A crate **70 cm × 1.50 m** — slightly larger than a coffin. He lived there from c. early 1942 through July 1944. He ate inside it. He stayed silent inside it during the day while his mother worked at the art-objects shop on Rynek Square. At night he slept in his mother's bed. When neighbours came over, Lusia placed a phonograph on top of the crate; music masked his sounds. His complexion went white from years without sunlight.

### Step 5 — David, hidden separately

> *"Mumek, her husband and Shimon's father, did not live with them. He lived somewhere in Lvov and 'improved' his outward appearance: grew a mustache and changed his hairstyle. ... Mumek, who worked in building railroad tracks, told her that suspicion had fallen on him and his friend Pesach because they were Jews, and therefore they were forced to bathe with the gentiles. They had to hide the fact that they were circumcised, and they did this in various and strange ways. Mumek, for example, would sew his male member to himself ..."* — page 46

David lived separately under his own Polish disguise (mustache + changed hair). He worked on railroad-track construction with another circumcised Jew named **Pesach**. Both surgically sewed back their circumcision to pass the public bath inspections.

## Why the previous inference (convent / Żegota) was wrong but not absurd

The reasoning chain that led to the convent hypothesis was demographically sound:
- ~250,000 Polish-Jewish survivors total
- Circumcised boys age 4-7 could rarely pass on the Aryan side
- Lwów had dense Polish-Jewish rescue networks (Żegota Lwów under Władysława Choms; Albertine Sisters; Studite Sisters under Sheptytsky)

So the convent hypothesis was the BAYESIAN PRIOR. The memoir is the DIRECT EVIDENCE. The memoir wins, decisively.

The Lwów convent / Żegota research is preserved as historical context in headline_finds card `lwow_convents_researched_not_applicable`. It remains useful for the LLM narrator to understand the broader rescue landscape and how the Rapaport family's choice fits — they chose to keep Shimon WITH his mother under extreme constraint rather than entrust him to outsiders. That is a meaningful family choice and a remarkable feat of survival.

## What this opens up for next research

1. **The Hormak family of Nadwórna** — they smuggled Shimon, transported Lusia, sheltered her with their Lwów-resident sister. They almost certainly qualify for Righteous Among the Nations recognition if not already designated. Search:
   - https://righteous.yadvashem.org/ — surname "Hormak" / "Hurmak" + place "Nadwórna"
   - Polscy Sprawiedliwi: https://sprawiedliwi.org.pl/en

2. **Lota Rapaport** (David's sister) — appears in the Lwów ghetto in 1942-43. Her ultimate fate is not yet documented in the archive. Search:
   - Yad Vashem Names DB for "Lota Rapaport" / "Lotka Rapaport" Lwów

3. **Pesach** (David's friend / co-worker on railroad construction) — circumcised Jew passing as gentile. Surname unknown. Would be a closing detail if found.

4. **Shimon's own testimony** — Chapter 7 page 83 of the memoir is "About Shimon" and describes his post-war life (school newspaper, Bamachane military weekly, almost on submarine Dakar, Ma'ariv newspaper Tiberias and Haifa). The crate experience was retold by Lusia, not Shimon — does any Shimon-authored account survive? Was he interviewed by Yad Vashem's Visual History Archive?

## For the LLM narrator

When narrating Shimon's wartime childhood, do NOT say he was in a convent. Say: he was hidden in a wooden crate inside his mother's apartment at Legionów 24 in Lwów, for somewhere between two and three years, never seeing sunlight, while a phonograph above his head played music to mask any sound he made. This is one of the most extraordinary survival stories in the family record, and it is documented in the mother's own voice.
