# Dossier 20 — Cyprus Karaolos camp + Theodor Herzl ship (verified)

**Verified 2026-05-21** · sources cross-checked against primary archives.

## 1. Theodor Herzl ship identity — verified

The Aliyah Bet ship "Theodor Herzl" that sailed from Sète on 2 April 1947 with 2,641 ma'apilim including David, Lusia, Shimon, and infant Dov Rapaport was:

- Built **1907** by Swan Hunter & Wigham Richardson in Newcastle, England
- Originally the British cable-laying ship **HMS/SS "Guardian"**
- **1,768 tons**, triple-expansion steam engine, ~10 knots cruising speed
- Acquired in **1946** by Mossad LeAliyah Bet
- Refitted at **Marseille** for ~2,600 passengers (standing-only deck accommodation)
- Sailed Sète **2 April 1947** with 2,641 ma'apilim from camps in France and Belgium
- Intercepted **13 April 1947** by HMS St Brides Bay and HMS Haydon
- **3 ma'apilim killed** (Aharon Dov, Pinchas Weiss, Menachem Samet); 27 wounded
- All passengers deported to Cyprus

**Critical disambiguation:** This is NOT the SS Exodus 1947 (which was the ex-SS President Warfield, built 1928 by Pusey & Jones in Wilmington, Delaware, and left Sète in July 1947). The two ships are routinely conflated in popular sources — the Theodor Herzl was a smaller, older British ship.

## 2. Cyprus internment camps — Karaolos vs Dekhelia

The British ran two camp clusters in Cyprus 1946-1949:

| Cluster | Location | Structure | Camp numbers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Caraolos / Karaolos** | North of Famagusta | Summer tent camps | 55, 60, 61, 62, 63 |
| **Dekhelia / Decauville** | Near Larnaca (~50 km from Karaolos) | Winter Nissen huts | — |

- 9 camps at peak across both sites
- **53,510 Jews** detained 1946-1949
- ~2,200 children born in captivity
- David's family was held **~8 months**
- Wounded passengers and unaccompanied minors diverted to **Atlit** in British Mandate Palestine instead of Cyprus

## 6. Bad Gastein → Belgium → Sète itinerary — DROPPED (implausible)

A prior research thread suggested David's family transited Bad Gastein (Austria) en route to Sète. Re-verification rules this out:

- Bad Gastein was a real Jewish DP camp 1946-47 in Salzburg, Austria, AND a Bricha node
- BUT Bad Gastein fed ma'apilim **southward via Italy/Tyrol to Italian Mediterranean ports** — Trieste, La Spezia, Bari — not westward through Belgium
- The Theodor Herzl's passengers came from **camps in France and Belgium** (per Palyam)
- A Brussels-based family with an infant born there in 1946 most plausibly travelled: **Belgium → southern France transit camp → Sète**, with **no Austrian leg**
- "Chateau de Rue" Belgium reference could not be located in USHMM/EHRI/OSE listings — likely mis-transcribed; possibly an OSE/AIVG children's home but unverified

Decision: **NOT embedded in the archive as fact.** Held in this dossier as a thread for future investigation.

Sources for ruling out: https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/bad-gastein-displaced-persons-camps · https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/berihah
