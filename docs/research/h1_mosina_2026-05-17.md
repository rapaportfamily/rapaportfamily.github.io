## Detailed findings

### Mszana Dolna — the negative evidence

Source: [Sztetl Mszana Dolna — The life](https://www.sztetlmszanadolna.com/en/the-life.html) (dedicated Jewish-heritage site for Mszana Dolna)

The page enumerates Jewish families in Mszana Dolna pre-WWII by occupation. Complete list of **timber-trade families**:

> Goldberger, Geller, Wiener, Feuerstein, Kleinmann, Frey, Wachtel, Weinberg, Faber.

Cross-checked against full surname list on the page (merchants, craftsmen, professionals, hospitality): **Rapaport / Rapoport / Rappaport does not appear anywhere.**

The same site has descendant pages for the [Rebhun family](https://www.sztetlmszanadolna.com/en/the-rebhun-family.html) and [Rotkopf family](https://www.sztetlmszanadolna.com/en/visit-of-rotkopf-family-descendants-in-mszana-dolna.html) — but no Rapaport descendant page exists.

**Interpretation**: If the "Rapaports of Mszana" oral memory referred to a family that owned a sawmill there in the 1930s, they would almost certainly appear on this site. Their absence is a strong (not conclusive) negative.

**What would change my mind**: A direct Yizkor-book reference to a Rapaport sawmill in Mszana Dolna would override the absence on Sztetl. Doron / Basia should access the [JewishGen Mszana Dolna Yizkor Book Project](https://bloodandfrogs.com/compendium/poland/lesser-poland/mszana-dolna/jewishgen-yizkor-book-project-mszana-dolna) (requires free JewishGen membership).

### Morszyn — confirmed context, no new family evidence

Source: [Morshyn Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morshyn), [NieznanaUkraina.pl](https://www.nieznanaukraina.pl/en/97/morszyn/)

- Polish spa town 1919–1939, in Stanisławów Voivodeship near Stryj
- Owned by Medical Association of Lviv; ~1000 guests/year by late 1920s
- Marketed as "Galician Karlsbad"
- Now Morshyn, Lviv Oblast, Ukraine

No Rapaport family appears in any Morszyn-specific source I could access. **Status: still a candidate, but currently unsupported by web sources.** Would need direct archive work (Lviv State Archive, Polish State Archive Lviv branch).

### Muszyna / Krynica — Jewish hospitality industry yes, Rapaport no

Sources: [Virtual Shtetl Muszyna](https://sztetl.org.pl/en/towns/m/1209-muszyna/99-history/137715-history-of-community), [Jewish community in Krynica](https://m.hotelprezydent.com/en/jewish-community-in-krynica-page-103526)

- Muszyna had Jewish-owned pensions in the 1930s, most famously "Bristol" owned by **Chaim Weiss**'s family
- Krynica had **6 out of 10** B&B entities Jewish-owned in interwar period; thousands of Jewish guests yearly
- No specific Rapaport hotelier / pensioner appears in available sources

**Status: still candidates** (the "rental hotel for Polish visitors" oral memory fits the area's profile) but no direct family attribution.

### Bolechów area — checked the Yizkor for context

Source: [Bolekhov Yizkor book, pp. 117–144](https://jewishgen.org/yizkor/bolekhov/bol117.html)

Covers Soviet rule and Holocaust years 1939–1944. Surnames cataloged: Reisman, Schindler, Gross, Hauftman, Sobel, Reifeisen, Pressler, Friedman, Landau, Horowitz, Bruckenstein, Bornstein, and many more. **None of Rapaport/Weitzner/Weinreb/Griffel/Chajes appear in this chapter.**

Caveat: this is one chapter of one book covering one period. The rest of the Bolekhov Yizkor and other chapter ranges may have references. Worth a fuller pass.

## Bonus finding — Lwów Rapaport prominent family branches (relevant to H10)

Source: [JewishGen Lvov Yizkor pages](https://www.jewishgen.org/yizkor/lviv/lvi525.html), [Lviv Interactive — old Jewish cemetery](https://lia.lvivcenter.org/en/objects/old-jewish-cemetery/)

Lwów had a long-established Rapaport rabbinic and professional dynasty:

- **Rabbi Chaim Rappaport** (d. 1771) — buried in the old Jewish cemetery; Vul. Rappaporta street in Lviv likely named after him
- **Dr. Jakubka Rappaport** — co-founder of the Reform Temple
- **Dr. Artur Rapaport** — lecturer in Classical Languages, Lwów University
- **Yancze Rapaport** — Vice-President of Lwów Judenrat during the Holocaust
- A **Gesher Galicia "Family Finder" entry** for Rapaport in Lwów was added 2022 by an unnamed member — Doron could contact via Gesher Galicia membership to see if this is a related branch

**Note for our David**: he moved to Lwów as a young forestry engineer (pre-1938). His family origins are firmly **Nadwórna** (Griffel maternal line via the breakthrough 15 May 2026 birth record). The prominent Lwów Rapaports above are not from his nuclear family but may be distant cousins worth tracing for H10.
