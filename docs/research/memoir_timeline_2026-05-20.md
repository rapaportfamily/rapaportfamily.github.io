# Lusia's memoir — timeline + cross-reference (2026-05-20)

**Source**: 90-page Hebrew memoir, OCR'd 2026-05-20, translated to English. Analyzed by Claude claude-haiku-4-5.

**Overall**: The memoir reveals Lusia's extraordinary resourcefulness and psychological resilience in surviving the Holocaust by successfully passing as a Polish woman while hiding her young son in a wooden box for over a year in Lwów under constant Nazi surveillance. The narrative confirms that David and Lusia were separated during much of the war (David in the Nadwórna ghetto until his escape; Lusia passing as 'Maria' in Lwów), and provides detailed accounts of their post-war journey through Brussels, Cyprus detention camps, and eventual resettlement in Israel. The memoir also establishes the critical role of non-Jewish helpers (Pietrozycki, Ivanov, the Hormak family, and Maria Ciccelik) in the family's survival, countering narratives that overlook gentile assistance during the Holocaust.

## Themes

- Maternal instinct and survival
- Identity and deception
- Kindness of strangers
- Resilience and hope
- Memory and testimony
- Separation and reunion

### 1937-06-22 · Shimon Rapaport born in Moszyna (Muszyna)  (memoir p. None)

Shimon (Szymon) Rapaport born to David and Leah in Moszyna, a small resort town in Poland.

> People: p_shimon, p_david, p_leah · Resolves/relates to: e_shimon_birth

### 1938 · Lusia becomes a mother and hotel manager in Moszyna  (memoir p. 17)

After Shimon's birth, Lusia decides to supplement family income by renting and managing a magnificent hotel in Moszyna. The hotel caters mostly to Polish guests, with few Jewish patrons.

> People: p_leah

### 1938 · Lusia rescues two drowning children from the river in Moszyna  (memoir p. 18)

While bathing in the river with young Shimon nearby, Lusia rescues two girls (ages 4 and 5) from the water. She later learns they are Christian children, and their mother sends flowers in thanks. This incident deepens Lusia's awareness of antisemitism.

> People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1938-02 · David and Leah marry in Moszyna  (memoir p. 16)

David Rapaport and Leah Weitzner marry in a 'white wedding' in the small resort town of Moszyna on a cold winter day with heavy snowfall. Leah is 18 years old, David is 24. They marry without family members present.

> People: p_david, p_leah · Resolves/relates to: e_david_leah_marriage

### 1939 · Lusia travels with Shimon to Przemyśl  (memoir p. 23)

After war breaks out, Lusia and young Shimon leave Moszyna to stay with David's maternal grandparents in Przemyśl. David follows later, arriving with only a radio receiver; he leaves house keys with the maid. Lusia finds work as a cashier/document writer for Soviet N.K.V.D. security police.

### 1939-09-01 · World War II begins—Germany invades Poland  (memoir p. 22)

Germany invades Poland, marking the start of World War II. News reaches Moszyna and David suggests Lusia travel with Shimon to his parents' home until 'the anger passes.'

> People: p_leah, p_shimon, p_david · Resolves/relates to: e_war_begins

### 1939-1940 · Lusia works for Soviet N.K.V.D. in Przemyśl  (memoir p. 24)

Soviet occupation of eastern Poland. Lusia is employed by the N.K.V.D., handling passports and documents, with access to safes containing confiscated Jewish jewelry and valuables.

> Places: pl_przemysl · People: p_leah

### 1940 · Lusia's grandmother murdered by Germans in Przemyśl  (memoir p. 24)

Lusia's grandmother (David's mother Rebeka/Regina) is killed by Germans, who accuse her of placing a bomb on the railway near Przemyśl.

> Places: pl_przemysl · People: p_leah, p_rebeka

### 1940 · Rapaport family moves to Nadwórna  (memoir p. 25)

Lusia, David, and Shimon relocate to Nadwórna (now Nadvirna, Ukraine), where David finds work at a sawmill. They live in a small house in a neighborhood of worker families, with gardens for growing vegetables.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah, p_david, p_shimon

### 1941 · German invasion of Soviet-held territories; Russians abandon Nadwórna  (memoir p. 25)

Russians, fearing German takeover, set fire to railway cars full of grain and provisions at Nadwórna station. Antisemitism intensifies as Poles blame Jews for collaborating with the Russians.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah

### 1941 · Ukrainians demand Jews wash corpses of murdered Poles  (memoir p. 26)

Corpses of murdered Poles are found in the forest near Nadwórna. Jews are accused and ordered to wash the bodies and drink the water used for washing—a humiliating decree.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah

### 1941 · Lusia and David flee to forest at night  (memoir p. 26)

Two Ukrainian collaborators arrive at Lusia's door demanding she come with them. Speaking Polish fluently and appearing as a gentile, she convinces them she is not Jewish; they leave. That night, with help from neighbor Pietrozycki, Lusia and David hide in a pit in the forest while Pietrozycki cares for Shimon.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah, p_david

### 1941 · German decrees against Jews in Nadwórna  (memoir p. 27)

Decrees published requiring Jews to wear yellow badges, surrender all gold, silver, and furs, and prohibiting travel by train without permission. David receives an 'essential worker' badge due to his sawmill employment.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah, p_david

### 1941 · Massacre of Hungarian and Czech Jews in Nadwórna  (memoir p. 29)

Multitudes of Jews fleeing Hungary and Czech territories arrive in Nadwórna seeking safety. Germans assemble them near the sawmill, where a three-day massacre ensues. Jews are tortured, shot, and thrown into a mass pit visible from Lusia's house. Only three survive.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah

### 1941 · Neighbor Ivanov warns Lusia of planned action against children  (memoir p. 30)

Russian gentile neighbor Ivanov, whose life Lusia had helped save during Soviet occupation, brings her warning that Germans plan to take Jewish children away. He helps arrange for Shimon to be sent to Lusia's sister-in-law in Lwów dressed in girls' clothing.

> Places: pl_nadworna, pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1941 · Nadwórna ghetto established  (memoir p. 31)

Ghetto established in Nadwórna. Public notice requires all Jews to transfer to designated houses within a set deadline (11:00 a.m. on a specific day) or face execution. Each family receives a single small room. David receives worker's tag allowing him to leave the ghetto to work.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah, p_david

### 1941 · Lusia refuses to enter ghetto; begins passing as Polish woman  (memoir p. 31)

Lusia decides not to enter the Nadwórna ghetto. With encouragement from gentile neighbors who note her fair hair and fluent Polish, she begins passing as a Polish woman and obtains forged health-fund certificate with forged signature.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_leah

### 1941 · Lusia walks to train station; travels to Lwów with Hormak family  (memoir p. 32)

Lusia walks 20 km to Nadwórna train station dressed as a Polish woman, carrying soap and food to trade. She successfully passes through checkpoints with her forged health-fund certificate. At the station, a member of the Hormak family (dealer in Jewish property) helps her obtain tickets and they travel together to Lwów.

> Places: pl_nadworna, pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1941 · Lusia arrives in Lwów; reunites with Shimon  (memoir p. 36)

Lusia arrives late at night in Lwów, where 110,000 Jews live. She stays with the sister of the Hormak family and is reunited with Shimon the next morning. Horror rumors spread that children will be executed; Lusia resolves to act quickly to save her son.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1941 · Shimon smuggled out of Lwów ghetto on 90th day  (memoir p. 39)

Lusia witnesses trucks of children being taken from the ghetto (pretext: so parents can work; reality: to certain death). She decides this is the moment to extract Shimon. She dresses him in girls' clothing and, with great confidence, walks him out of the ghetto gates with her. She then brings him to Mrs. Inovska (Hormak's sister) and, after careful consideration, confesses Shimon is her Jewish son, begging for mercy to save him.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1941 · David escapes Nadwórna ghetto; reunites with Lusia at Lwów ghetto gate  (memoir p. 41)

David escapes Nadwórna ghetto using a plan devised by neighbor Ivanov: hiding among wooden planks in train cars destined for transport. He and 17 others drill holes in thick wood. When liquidation of the ghetto begins, they hide; one person had informed on the plan, so they jump from the moving train. David manages to reach Lwów and meets Lusia at the gate of the Lwów ghetto on Rappoport Street.

> Places: pl_nadworna, pl_lwow · People: p_david, p_leah · Resolves/relates to: h_david_war_escape

### 1941 · Lusia finds apartment and work in Lwów; hides Shimon in wooden box  (memoir p. 43)

Lusia finds an apartment at Legionow Street 24 (opposite the theater) that had belonged to Jews who moved to the ghetto. She arranges a hiding place for Shimon inside a narrow wooden box (70 cm × 1.50 m) used for storing potatoes and vegetables. She creates an entrance by removing boards connecting the box to the wall.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1941 · Lusia obtains forged documents; becomes 'Maria' at Maritzka Square  (memoir p. 43)

Using forged documents identifying her as 'Maria,' a Pole, Lusia finds work managing a hat shop on Maritzka Square, a major commercial center in Lwów run by German manager Donner. Most shops are Jewish-owned. She speaks German with a Polish accent during the examination.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1941-1942 · Lusia promoted to manager of art objects shop  (memoir p. 44)

Donner promotes 'Maria' to manage a larger, more prominent shop selling art objects and handicrafts from rural Polish artisans. The shop becomes a gathering place. A young Polish underground girl works there, hiding underground newspapers that publish information about German atrocities and appeals to Poles not to harm Jews.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1941-1942 · Shimon confined to wooden box; David sutures himself to hide circumcision  (memoir p. 46)

Shimon lives in the wooden box, eating, playing, sleeping, and doing his needs inside. He does not leave the box all day for many months. Shimon is pale and understands the danger, asking his mother to 'do something so I won't be Jewish.' David, who lives separately, sterilizes a needle with fire and sutures his male member to hide the fact of circumcision, fearing discovery by Germans at bathing stations. Friend Pesach cannot do this himself, so Lusia performs the suturing for him.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon, p_david

### 1941-1942 · Lusia encounters gymnast classmate; maintains cover  (memoir p. 47)

A Polish girl whom Lusia studied with in gymnasium recognizes her at the shop. Lusia calmly explains she is a Polish friend, speaking with forced composure about how beautiful the shop is and how pleasant the work is.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1941-1942 · Lusia obtains Gestapo permission to travel to Zakopane  (memoir p. 47)

Donner invites 'Maria' to accompany him on a trip to Zakopane in the mountains to purchase art objects from Polish peasants. She needs Gestapo permission to travel in a car reserved for Germans. At Gestapo headquarters, a tall, imposing Gestapo officer interrogates her intensely. She answers in clear Polish with confidence despite terror. The officer grants permission and remarks on her excellent German.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1942 · Lusia assists Dr. Korner's daughter; gives watch to informant  (memoir p. 48)

Lusia encounters the 14-year-old daughter of Dr. Korner, a dentist murdered by Germans for possessing gold. The girl is dressed in rags. Lusia helps her with food and clothing and gives her address. A large man later stops Lusia on the street, identifying her as 'Mrs. Rapaport.' He reveals he was sent by the Criminal Police ('Kriminal Polizei') with evidence against her. Lusia gives him a watch with 10 dollars inside. He promises to tell the Criminal Police that information about her is false and that she is Polish, not Jewish.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1942 · Criminal Police detectives trail Lusia; she maintains composure  (memoir p. 50)

Lusia notices men's footsteps behind her on her way to work. She thinks 'This is it! My path ends here!' But she gathers her strength and mighty will to live, increases her pace, reaches the shop, and opens it as usual. Two men enter and say 'We suspect you are not a Pole.' Lusia calmly hands them her documents. Everything is correct—a Polish document without flaw. The men examine the documents, look at each other, and leave, but great anxiety remains with Lusia.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1942 · Maria Ciccelik visits from Nadwórna; Gestapo raids apartment  (memoir p. 51)

Maria Ciccelik, the real Polish woman whose birth certificate Lusia forged documents from, visits Lwów for Christmas with her young daughter (about Shimon's age). While they sit talking, two men in civilian clothes burst in, identifying themselves as Criminal Police. They demand documents. The real Maria (a peasant woman with black hair and no documents) remains silent when asked her name. When she finally gives it, the Germans say 'both of you will be destroyed.' They march to the box asking what is inside; Lusia answers 'Laundry.' They open it, find Shimon, pull him out, interrogate him. He remains silent, unmoved even when hit with a rifle butt. Lusia, believing they will all die, falls to her knees begging them to spare Maria and her daughter. She gives them her powder compact with 10 dollars. The Germans exchange an astonished look and decide 'she does not have a Jewish character'—they leave.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1942-1943 · Lusia sends Shimon to Nadwórna with Maria Ciccelik  (memoir p. 53)

After the Gestapo raid, Lusia decides it is better for Shimon to leave. Maria Ciccelik offers to take him to Nadwórna. Lusia dresses Shimon in girls' clothing (he looks like 'a small, sweet girl'). She hires a truck to transport Maria, her daughter, and Shimon to Nadwórna on a harsh winter night with biting cold and heavy rains. Shimon remains with Maria in Nadwórna for the rest of the war.

> Places: pl_lwow, pl_nadworna · People: p_leah, p_shimon

### 1942-1944 · Criminal Police visit again; Lusia offers hospitality and false confession  (memoir p. 54)

Several months after Shimon's departure, Criminal Police knock on Lusia's door again. She offers them food and drink while they search the now-empty box. She falsely confesses that the Jewess who had stayed with her (Maria Ciccelik) was kind-hearted and that she, Lusia, had made a 'terrible mistake' hiding the child out of gratitude, but she has since expelled them. With 'feminine cunning,' she makes them believe the story. They finish eating and leave. Lusia leans against the door with her forehead touching it, breathing deeply, hoping this time they will finally leave her alone.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1942-1944 · Lusia continues life of constant tension in Lwów  (memoir p. 57)

Lusia lives in continuous tension. She attends church every Sunday as cover for being 'a devout Polishwoman.' She rarely sees her husband, who lives in a small hiding place near a factory. She witnesses daily scenes of horror: Germans seizing young men and beating them; a wounded Jewish man bleeding on the sidewalk at the market begging for water, which she cannot help. She refrained from approaching him to avoid being caught, but tears flowed from her eyes as he bled.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah, p_david

### 1944 · Black soldier breaks into building; Lusia gives him clothes  (memoir p. 57)

Late in the war, a black soldier (very dirty, with rifle) breaks into Lusia's building and demands that those in hiding raise their hands and reveal German positions. Speaking Russian, Lusia warns him that if he appears as he is, Germans will shoot him and his friends will shoot everyone in the building. She offers to provide him other clothes and a scarf. He promises not to shoot near the house. The next day his body is found riddled with bullets.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1944 · Germans abandon Lwów; Russians take control  (memoir p. 57)

After the black soldier's death, the Germans abandon Lwów and Russian forces take their place, ending the Nazi occupation of the city.

> Places: pl_lwow · People: p_leah

### 1944 · Shimon becomes ill in Nadwórna; family fears for his life  (memoir p. 58)

While Shimon lives hidden with Maria in Nadwórna, he becomes seriously ill with high fever and difficult cough. His life is in danger. The family caring for him fears where to bury him if he dies. Shimon mumbles from his sickbed, pleading 'Jesus, do something so I will not be a Jew.' A solution is devised to bury him in the garden among flowers if necessary. To everyone's joy, Shimon recovers.

> Places: pl_nadworna · People: p_shimon

### 1945 · War ends; Lusia, David, and Shimon leave Poland for Katowice  (memoir p. 62)

With the end of World War II, Lusia, her husband David (Memek), and their son Shimon leave the Russian part of Poland (Lwów) and travel to Katowice as part of the repatriation process.

> Places: pl_lwow, pl_katowice · People: p_leah, p_david, p_shimon

### 1946 · Rapaport family arrives in Brussels; stays in hotel  (memoir p. 62)

Lusia, David, and Shimon travel from Katowice to Brussels by bus (journey takes place mainly at night to cross borders illegally). They are received by International Red Cross representatives. The family stays in a hotel with rent paid by the Jewish relief organization Joint.

> Places: pl_brussels · People: p_leah, p_david, p_shimon

### 1946 · Dov Bernard Rapaport born in Brussels  (memoir p. 62)

Dov (Dovek), the Rapaports' second son, is born in Brussels, Belgium in 1946/1947.

> Places: pl_brussels · People: p_leah, p_david, p_dov_bernard · Resolves/relates to: e_dov_birth

### 1946-04-09 · David registered as Displaced Person in Brussels  (memoir p. None)

David is registered as a Displaced Person in Brussels, Belgium, following the repatriation journey from Poland through illegal border crossings.

> Places: pl_brussels · People: p_david · Resolves/relates to: e_arrival_brussels

### 1947 · David and Shimon board ship Theodor Herzl for Palestine  (memoir p. 63)

Memek and Shimon join a journey organized by emissaries from the Land of Israel. They board the ship 'Theodor Herzl' with a small suitcase and many hopes. The ship is captured by the British fleet. All passengers are deported to refugee camps in Cyprus.

> People: p_david, p_shimon · Resolves/relates to: h_brussels_to_israel_route

### 1947 · Lusia remains in Brussels caring for infant Dov; finds work  (memoir p. 63)

Lusia remains in Brussels with three-month-old Dov while her husband and elder son are in Cyprus and Palestine. She finds work sewing linings for fur coats. The residence caretaker feeds and cares for Dov while she works. She receives an 'affidavit' (entry and residence permit) to the United States from her husband's family, the Dreier family, but refuses to separate from her husband and son and rejects the offer.

> Places: pl_brussels · People: p_leah, p_dov_bernard

### 1947 · Shimon released from Atlit detention camp; becomes registered immigrant  (memoir p. 65)

After about a year in a detention camp in Cyprus, Shimon is released and brought to the Land to a detention camp in Atlit, which serves as 'the corridor to freedom.' He is registered as an immigrant to Palestine.

> People: p_shimon

### 1948 · State of Israel established; War of Independence begins  (memoir p. 65)

The State of Israel is established and battles break out in the War of Independence. Lusia receives no news of Shimon and Memek, not knowing they are detained in Cyprus. She hears various rumors spread in Belgium about what is happening in the Land.

> People: p_leah

### 1948 · Lusia and Dov sail on ship Kadima to Haifa  (memoir p. 66)

With the establishment of the State of Israel, Lusia decides to ascend to be reunited with her husband and son. She and Dov sail on the ship 'Kadima' on a turbulent, difficult, stormy journey. The sea is rough and the ship sways violently. During one violent night, the door of her cabin comes loose and strikes her hard. The captain, seeing her covered in blood being comforted by her small child, promises her a free ticket for life on his ship.

> Places: pl_brussels · People: p_leah, p_dov_bernard

### 1948 · Lusia and Dov arrive in Haifa; reunite with parents  (memoir p. 67)

Lusia and Dov arrive at the port of Haifa with the ship 'Kadima' and descend straight to the home of her parents on Peace Street. Lusia gradually comes to understand this is not a dream. Her father reassures her when she sees a policeman: 'Here everyone is a Jew. The policemen are also Jews.'

> People: p_leah, p_dov_bernard

### 1948 · Lusia travels through regions of Israel with her father  (memoir p. 68)

In her first days in the Land, Lusia travels with her father through different regions of the country. A trip in the Sharon region is especially memorable—she sees green orchards full of citrus fruits (oranges, lemons, grapefruits, mandarins). The blossoming renewal of nature and stunning landscape stir her heart.

> People: p_leah

### 1948 · Rapaport family moves to Abbas Street apartment in Haifa  (memoir p. 68)

After some time, Lusia, Memek, and the two boys move to their own apartment on Abbas Street in Haifa. It is a small apartment with one room, small kitchen, and a toilet outside. Abbas Street is a binational street where Jews and Arabs live side by side. Lusia gets along excellently with her Arab neighbors.

> People: p_leah, p_david, p_shimon, p_dov_bernard

### 1950s · Rapaports purchase apartment on Moriah Street using reparations  (memoir p. 70)

Using reparations payments from Germany, Lusia and Memek purchase an apartment with two rooms, kitchen, and toilet on Moriah Street 93 on the Carmel in Haifa. Although on the ground floor below street level, Lusia is happy to live there.

> People: p_leah, p_david

### 1950s · Lusia and Memek find work in Israel  (memoir p. 70)

Memek works with his father-in-law in a small grocery store, then later in customs. Lusia works for 11 years in the post office in the department of telegrams abroad. She arrives at 7 a.m. without a single day of being late after waking early to prepare sandwiches for her children and standing at the bus stop at 6:30 a.m.

> People: p_leah, p_david

### circa 1958 · Lusia and Memek travel to West Berlin for Nazi reparations  (memoir p. 76)

At the end of the 1950s, Lusia and Memek travel to West Berlin to handle personal compensation as victims of Nazi persecution. Memek wrestles internally with his conscience—part of him cannot forgive the Germans, but another part argues it would be wrong to refuse legally and rightfully due payments for his father's factory, murdered family members, and suffering. In the end, he agrees to pursue the claim. The request for payment is submitted at the 90th minute (last day of deadline) and Lusia handwrites it in block letters.

> People: p_leah, p_david

### circa 1958 · Lusia meets young German lawyer; helps Shimon with reparations claim  (memoir p. 76)

In Berlin, Lusia meets a young German lawyer at the beginning of his career in the lobby of a Jewish hotel. She tells him Shimon's story and mentions that an Israeli lawyer claimed Shimon has no chance of approval. The lawyer 'lights up' and says Shimon is more entitled than anyone else. Lusia immediately signs a power of attorney in Shimon's name. After several years, this lawyer (now in a senior position in German judicial system) succeeds in obtaining reparations payments for Shimon.

> People: p_leah, p_shimon

### circa 1958 · Lusia travels alone to East Berlin to visit Hitler's grave  (memoir p. 78)

While in West Berlin, Lusia decides she must close the circle of the terrible years of her past by seeing Hitler's grave with her own eyes. She travels alone to East Berlin, where Hitler is buried. East Berlin is communist, still recovering from war damage with ruined buildings and destroyed infrastructure. At the grave marked only 'Hitler,' Lusia loses control, clenches her fists, spits on the grave repeatedly, and screams: 'You human monster, murderer like you, I, my husband and my children have remained alive while you lie here in a grave...' She spits and screams until exhausted, then closes her eyes and says in her heart that she will never erase this Holocaust from her memory.

> People: p_leah

### 1960s · Lusia begins volunteer work with B'nai B'rith  (memoir p. 72)

When the children grow up, Lusia begins volunteer activities in the 'Tachrichim' office of B'nai B'rith. She works with children aged 6-13 at a school in Tirat HaCarmel, bringing them sports equipment, organizing collective bar mitzvahs, and collecting funds through a bazaar in her home.

> People: p_leah

### 1960s · Lusia sent as delegate to B'nai B'rith convention in United States  (memoir p. 72)

Lusia is sent as a delegate to the 45th annual convention of B'nai B'rith held in the United States.

> People: p_leah

### 1986 · Lusia's granddaughter Hadas approaches teacher Esther Weiss  (memoir p. 4)

In summer 1986, Hadas (Lusia's granddaughter and Shimon's daughter) approaches her teacher Esther Weiss and tells her that 'Grandma Lusia' survived the Holocaust and has many stories, but is unable to write them down. Hadas requests that the teacher help collect these stories.

> People: p_leah, p_hadas_rapaport

### 1986 · Teacher Esther Weiss conducts interviews with Lusia  (memoir p. 4)

Over several meetings lasting many hours, teacher Esther Weiss visits Lusia's private apartment and collects stories from her about her life during World War II and the Holocaust, not always in chronological order. Weiss produces a booklet called 'Lusia' based on these interviews.

> People: p_leah
