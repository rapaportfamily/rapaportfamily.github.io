## 🏨 Hotel "Willa Helin" — Lusia's Muszyna hotel identified (Basia, 21 May 20:58)

- **Name**: Willa "Helin"
- **Address**: Ogrodowa Street, Muszyna
- **Cited in**: "The Birth of Muszyna's Spa" (Stanisław Heilman & Jerzy Krzyś) — listed alongside "Zosia" and "Aleksandrówka" as one of the brick comfort buildings
- **Fate**: Seized by Gestapo. Converted to torture chamber for captured Polish couriers (incl. famous skier Helena Marusarzówna). Plaque unveiled 1992.
- **Implication**: "The Rapaports had nothing to return to in Muszyna" — the hotel was a war-crime site post-1939.

### Sawmill identity — Zygmunt Griffel front company

- Rafał Żebrowski article ("Jews in Muszyna and the Holocaust") attributed Muszyna sawmill to "**Segal-Żupnik-Wilf**"
- 1938 *Przegląd Drzewny* ("Export of sets to Palestine") lists "**Zygmunt Griffel Lwów (Segal, Żupnik, and Wilf)**" — same entity
- **Conclusion**: The Muszyna sawmill was Zygmunt Griffel's, operating under a holding-company name. Lusia's hotel + the Griffel sawmill are confirmed to share Muszyna location.
- 28.10.1933 Muszyna Commune Council minutes: Zygmunt Griffel Company won timber contract at 8.40 PLN/m³ over Chastel Horowitz of Tylicz.
- **Zygmunt Griffel was son of Dawid Mendel Griffel** (= Rebeka's brother → David's maternal uncle).
