New York
Jewish Communal Register
Nadworna Listings
1917-1918

The following are entries for Nadworna institutions found in The Jewish Communal Register of New York City, 1917-1918, edited and published by the Kehillah (Jewish Community) of New York City.  Second edition. 1918.

Mutual Aid Agencies in Manahattan and The Bronx

First Nadworner K.U.V. (F.G.B.J.A): pages 765-6

Sick Benefit; cemetery; place of worship
Organized 1897
Membership: 135
Meetings: 1st and 3rd Sundays, at 62 E. 4th St. 
President , Harry Yager, 751 E. 5th St.
Secretary, Abraham Traub, 68 E. 1st St.


Yager, Harry, President First Nadworner K.U.V.
(62 E. 4th St.)
since 1914
Term 6 months
Born 1870 in Austria
Came to U.S. 1904
Received general Jewish education
Residence: 751 E. 5th St.

First Nadworner Y.M.B.A. (F.G.B.J.A): page 766

Sick Benefit; cemetery
Organized 1910
Membership: 40
Meetings: 1st and 3rd Tuesdays, at 159 Rivington St. 
President, Harry Shuster, 197 Allen St.
Secretary, D. Werner, 381 Alabama Ave., B'klyn.

Shuster, Harry, President First Nadworner Y.M.B.A.
(159 Rivington St.)
elected 1917
Term 6 months
Born 1885 in Austria
Came to U.S. 1904
Received general Jewish and secular education
Residence: 197 Allen St.
